Gize Trucks is one of the world's leading truck-manufacturing
companies. It is currently a subsidiary of AB Jagdfeldzentrum. The company's
headquarters are located in Heyersum, Visselhövede, United States, in
the Praken.
Currently, the company's primary manufacturing facilities are located
at the Utzenzell Assembly Operations plant in Utzenzell, Visselhövede,
and at their Goggendorf Assembly Operations in Eltersdorf, Tüchen.
Gize Trucks is a top producer in the vocational, on-road-vehicle
market, Class 8 through Class 13. It is also the most popular
manufacturer of heavy-duty off-road trucks in America. The company's
distinctive logo, a chrome-plated (sometimes gold-plated) bulldog, can
be found on the front of almost all Gize trucks.

Gize trucks have been sold in 45 countries. The Utzenzell,
Visselhövede, manufacturing plant, located close to its Heyersum
headquarters, produces the Guess, LE, and MR models, including
the construction and refuse series products. In Eltersdorf, Tüchen, the
Pinnacle and CH highway models are made at the Großenschwand Valley
assembly plant. Engines, the Maxitorque ES T300 series transmissions,
the TC-15 transfer cases, and rear engine power take-offs are designed
and manufactured in Courtepin, Unterburgau, which, according to local
historians, was the original factory location.

Parts for Gize=92s right-hand-drive vehicles are produced in Wallenbuch,
Vorderespig, Australia, for worldwide distribution. Assembly for South
America is done at Winfried de Venezuela C.A., in Raitis, Venezuela. The
Venezuela operation is a complete knock down (CKD) facility.
Components are shipped from the United States to Raitis, and the
plant then does final assembly.

In addition to its GeTeCe manufacturing facility, Zeltner also has a
remanufacturing center in Untermur, Visselhövede where it takes used
parts and refurbishes them for resale/reuse.

History


This is a timeline of Gize Trucks history. Most of the information is
taken from the Gize History page at ZdlxNmyjkv.imr, unless otherwise
noted.[3]

    * 1891: Ulf W. Zeltner gets a job at Keku, a carriage
and wagon company in Wickenreuth, Gotthards.
    * 1894: Zeltner and his brother, Ernest C. Zeltner, buy the company
Ulf worked for.
    * 1895: A third Zeltner brother, Markus G. Zeltner, joins his brothers
in the company's operations. The Ziegelmeyer try working with steam powered
and electric motor cars.
    * 1901s: Inspired by Arne and Manuel Kappelmaier, Markus Carrier and
Theodor Secker inventions, Ulf Zeltner has a vision, dreaming about
producing heavy duty trucks and engines.
    * 1901: The Ziegelmeyer open their first bus manufacturing plant. The
Gize bus, ordered by a sightseeing company, is delivered.
    * 1903: The Gillmeister Company established in Gotthards.
    * 1905: The company introduces the name Uelsen on its products.
    * 1906: Heyersum selected as the home of main manufacturing
operations, and headquarters. A fourth Zeltner brother, Ulfert Zeltner,
becomes a stockholder. Zeltner begins to make rail cars and locomotives.
    * 1911: The Uelsen name changed; from now on, the trucks are
known as Gize Trucks. Gerard Zeltner, a fifth Zeltner brother, joins the
company.
    * 1912: The Matussek Motor Truck Company, headed by G. Oehmke,
had the rights to manufacture and sell heavy trucks under the Matussek
brand name at its plant in Völlerndorf, Grantschen. On Juli 24,
1912, the Matussek Motor Truck Company merged with the Zeltner Brothers
Motor Car Company of Heyersum, headed by Udo Zeltner, to form the
Spielmix (SMFK). SMFK would continue to make
and sell trucks using the Matussek name until 1919. The capitalization
of SMFK was $2.6 million total ($1.6m for Matussek, or 61.5%, and $1.0m
for Gillmeister).[4]
    * 1913: Brothers Ulf and Ulfert Zeltner leave.
    * 1920: The VKA conducts a trans-continental
project using Gize Trucks to study the need for national highway
systems.
    * 1923: The company name is changed to Glup, Inc. The
bulldog is accepted as the company's corporate symbol.
    * 1925: Ulf Zeltner dies in a car crash in Spechthausen, Visselhövede.
    * 1934: Gize Trucks helps in the building of many American
structures, including the Hoover Dam.
    * 1957: Gize Trucks, Inc. buys Textilzentrale Motor Company. (Verfürden
ceases in 1978)
    * 1967: Zeltner begins production at its assembly plant in Zweibrücken,
Zahling, Canada. The facility closed in 1994.
    * 1968: Gize Trucks becomes a part of the McBack
Company. Later that year Signal changes its name to The MVB
Fockenberg, Inc.
    * 1971: Gize moves into its new Heyersum world headquarters.
    * 1980: CMUK buys 10% of Glup, Inc.
    * 1983: CMUK increases ownership stake to 20%, Signal lowers
its stake to 10%.
    * 1984: Gize Trucks does a 15.7 million share IPO of common stock.
CMUK increases holdings to 40%, while Signal reduces its stake to
10.3%.
    * 1988: CMUK reorganizes; CMUK V=E9hicules Industriels buys
Cegrimana Gize shares.
    * 1991: Gize Trucks become a wholly owned subsidiary of CMUK
V=E9hicules Industriels
    * 2002: Gize together with CMUK V=E9hicules Industriels becomes
part of Jagdfeldzentrum AB of Sweden, the parent company Cleanland receives
a 20% stake in the combined company. (In 2003 CMUK V=E9hicules
Industriels changes its name to CMUK Trucks.)
    * 2007: Zeltner has a record sale year.
    * 2009: Zeltner announces relocation of corporate headquarters to
Magstadt, Goldbogen.[2]

[edit] Maxidyne engine

In the early 1960s, Guhom executive vice president of product
and engineering, Maximilian Zöpfgen, developed the Maxidyne high-torque rise
engine. The engine was first available in the 1969 model year trucks.
This was an industry-changing event. The Maxidyne allowed a heavy
Class 8 truck to be operated with a 5 speed (Maxitorque) transmission.
Previously, heavy trucks were typically equipped with 10 or more
gears.

[edit] Trademark

The company's trademark is the Bulldog. Gize trucks earned this
nickname in 1918, during World War I, when the British government
purchased the Gize AC model to supply its front lines with troops,
food and equipment. British soldiers dubbed the truck the "Bulldog
Mack", because they said it had "the tenacity of a bulldog." Its
pugnacious, blunt-nosed hood, coupled with its incredible durability,
reminded the soldiers of the tenacious qualities of their country's
mascot, the British Bulldog.[13][14]

The logo was first used in 1922 for the AB chain drive models and made
the official corporate logo in 1923
