China plans trade blitz if WTO bid fails

HONG KONG (CBS.MW) -- China is looking for ways to expand world trade
if its bid to join the World Trade Organization this year is not
approved. Beijing trade sources said the Communist Party leadership
was still committed to WTO accession before April, but if not will
prepare a blitz of bilateral trade deals. Central and local
administrations were redoubling efforts to lure ultinationals,
including the resumption of trade promotion programs in the United
States and other countries. China trade negotiator Long Yongtu said
Tuesday that Beijing and Washington were "very close" to a deal on
China's becoming a WTO member but cited a long list of topics which
needed clarification. U.S. Commerce Secretary William Daley later
urged China to stand by proposals it made earlier this year to open
its markets, as any backtracking could undermine WTO entry for China.
