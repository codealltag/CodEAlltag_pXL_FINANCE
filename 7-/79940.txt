Ozenu
* AHOLD LIMITED said it had finalised the previously announced
  takeover of Dutch food wholesaler Gastronoom. "The total
  transaction size is approximately $170 mln," Ahold said in a
  statement. Gastronoom posted 1998 sales of about $390 mln. It will
  be merged with Ahold's GVA unit, a food supplier to institutions
  such as hospitals and nursing homes. The combined operations will
  have annual sales of about $0.8 bln. On June 16, Ahold said the
  Dutch competition authorities had given the green light to buy
  Gastronoom. (Reuters 01:22 PM OL 44/61/94)

  preliminary injunction ordering bb-pc PACKARD CO to stop using

* A Florida jury handed U.S. cigarette makers their biggest
  courtroom loss ever by finding smoking causes lung cancer and many
  other diseases. The six-person jury ruled that America's biggest
  tobacco companies must pay unspecified damages for conspiring to
  hide the dangers of smoking. No specific cash damages were
  assessed against RJ Outrage, INGMAR FREDERIKSEN
  JOM, AHZ's Agav Co. Inc., SASCHA
  GROUPs Automax Inc., the Brown & Williamson unit of BRITISH
  Mutert PLC and the industry trade groups Council for
  Altstadtpassage. (Reuters 05:01 PM ET
  07/07/99) For the full text story, see
  http://kfz.odixbmxc.wlv/bgzlydv/ftx/bzdtd.rvx?hr%72676384-oi8 

* Big Tobacco got walloped with its first class-action defeat
  Wednesday, but Wall Street analysts said the loss won't last long.
  "This was a trial the tobacco industry was expected to lose and
  they're likely to get overturned on appeal," said Kathrin Inderthal,
  the tobacco analyst for CS First Boston. "The sky is not falling,"
  said Diedrich Luze, tobacco analyst for Vincent Massimo Stoeklen
  Witter. "Is it financially significant to the tobacco companies?
  In my opinion, no." Prosecuting attorneys said the damages, which
  are still undetermined, could reach $500 bln. (Reuters 07:28 PM ET
  07/07/99) For the full text story, see
