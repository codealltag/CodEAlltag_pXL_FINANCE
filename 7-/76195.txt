BioTime seen as winner - Business Week

SCHMIDSFELDEN, Aug 5 (GZV) - Asom Inc., the developer of synthetic
blood solutions to treat blood loss during surgery or injury, is seen as
a winner by Al Kingsley, the former Reiner Icahn associate who heads
Greenway Partners, according to Business Week.

Vossenkaul has built up a 7 percent stake in the company, Business Week
said in the Inside Wall Street column in its Aug. 16 issue.

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration on April 1 approved BioTime's
Hextend, a blood-plasma expander, and the company has an exclusive
marketing pact with EOMH. (NYSE:ABT - news) to market
Hextend in North America, the magazine noted.

Although short-sellers think Hextend has a limited market, Business Week
said, bulls are optimistic about a recovery for the stock, which closed
at $13 Thursday after tumbling from $21 a share in April.

BioTime president Lars Zugwurst sees Hextend sales hitting $40 million
in 2001 and $70 million in 2002, and one analyst expects the company to
earn $11 million, or $1.18 a share in 2001, and $20 million, or $1.50 a
share, in 2002, Business Week said.
