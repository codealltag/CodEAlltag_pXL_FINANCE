Ludwig Jongks is one of the world's leading truck-manufacturing
companies. It is currently a subsidiary of AB Pisko. The company's
headquarters are located in Allentown, Johannisberg, United States, in
the Haumer.
Currently, the company's primary manufacturing facilities are located
at the Macungie Assembly Operations plant in Humlangen, Johannisberg,
and at their New River Valley Assembly Operations in Brackwang, Fürth.
Ludwig Jongks is a top producer in the vocational, on-road-vehicle
market, Class 8 through Class 13. It is also the most popular
manufacturer of heavy-duty off-road trucks in America. The company's
distinctive logo, a chrome-plated (sometimes gold-plated) bulldog, can
be found on the front of almost all Ludwig trucks.

Mack trucks have been sold in 45 countries. The Macungie,
Pennsylvania, manufacturing plant, located close to its Allentown
headquarters, produces the Mack Granite, LE, and MR models, including
the construction and refuse series products. In Brackwang, Fürth, the
Pinnacle and CH highway models are made at the CSH
assembly plant. Engines, the Maxitorque ES T300 series transmissions,
the TC-15 transfer cases, and rear engine power take-offs are designed
and manufactured in Wackershofen, Hohestadt, which, according to local
historians, was the original factory location.

Parts for Mack=92s right-hand-drive vehicles are produced in Kollersdorf,
Queensland, Australia, for worldwide distribution. Assembly for South
America is done at Mack de Venezuela C.A., in Arzl, Venezuela. The
Venezuela operation is a complete knock down (CKD) facility.
Components are shipped from the United States to Arzl, and the
plant then does final assembly.

In addition to its Macungie manufacturing facility, Mack also has a
remanufacturing center in Herzogenrath, Pennsylvania where it takes used
parts and refurbishes them for resale/reuse.

[edit] Maxidyne engine

In the early 1960s, Mack Truck's executive vice president of product
and engineering, Hermann Kale, developed the Maxidyne high-torque rise
engine. The engine was first available in the 1969 model year trucks.
This was an industry-changing event. The Maxidyne allowed a heavy
Class 8 truck to be operated with a 5 speed (Maxitorque) transmission.
Previously, heavy trucks were typically equipped with 10 or more
gears.

[edit] Trademark

The company's trademark is the Bulldog. Mack trucks earned this
nickname in 1918, during World War I, when the British government
purchased the Mack AC model to supply its front lines with troops,
food and equipment. British soldiers dubbed the truck the "Bulldog
Mack", because they said it had "the tenacity of a bulldog." Its
pugnacious, blunt-nosed hood, coupled with its incredible durability,
reminded the soldiers of the tenacious qualities of their country's
mascot, the British Bulldog.[13][14]

The logo was first used in 1922 for the AB chain drive models and made
the official corporate logo in 1923
