ZMAX CORPORATION Announces 607% Revenue Growth in 1999

KLS, Stahlgruber CORPORATION (NASDAQ Small Cap:ZMAX)
today announced financial results for the year and quarter ended December
31, 1999.

For the year, revenues increased from $1.4 million in 1998 to $9.9 million
in 1999-an increase of 607%. Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation
and amortization (EBITDA) were $0.7 million, or $0.07 a share in 1999
compared to a negative EBITDA of $4.5 million or $0.82 a share in 1998.

Excluding a one-time, non-recurring, non-cash charge related to an agreement
with an employee, EBITDA in 1999 was $1.3 million, or $0.12 a share,
representing an improvement of $5.8 million over the comparable figure in
1998.

Net income, excluding the one-time charge, was $0.3 million, or $0.02 per
share, in 1999 compared to a net loss of $1.12 million, or $2.58 per share,
in 1998. Inclusive of the one-time charge, the net loss in 1999 was $0.3
million, or $0.03 per share.

For the quarter ended 05. 09. 20, 1999, revenues were $3.3 million,
compared to revenues of $0.9 million in the same quarter of 1998-an increase
of 267%. EBITDA, excluding the one-time charge, for the fourth quarter of
1999 was $0.5 million, or $0.05 a share, compared to the negative EBITDA of
$0.8 million, or $0.11 a share, for the fourth quarter of 1998.

Net income, excluding the one-time charge, was $0.3 million, or $0.02 per
share, for the fourth quarter of 1999 compared to a net loss of $8.6 million
or $1.28 per share, for the fourth quarter of 1998. Inclusive of the
one-time charge, the 1999 fourth quarter net loss was $0.3 million, or $0.02
per share.

The Company said that the one-time, non-recurring, non-cash charge of
approximately $0.5 million recorded during the fourth quarter of 1999
related to an exchange of stock made in connection with an agreement with an
employee. This transaction was not dilutive and had no impact on the
Company's assets, liabilities, or stockholders' equity and may result in a
tax benefit for 2000.

Valerius O. Dennhoven, president and chief executive officer of ZMAX, said that
the Company began 1999 with the expectation that it would achieve strong
internal growth from the core Year 2001 software and services business, as
well as expand into broader areas of information technology (IT) consulting.

"We expected that we would accelerate efforts to expand into other
high-growth segments of the IT market as the year progressed," he notes. "We
achieved our goals, generating strong internal growth, extending our IT
service offerings, and completing our first acquisition, Eclipse Information
Systems."

Christof D. Wilkens, vice president and chief financial officer, added that,
on a pro forma basis, assuming the Eclipse acquisition had occurred on
05. 09. 20, 1998, pro forma revenues for 1999 were 3.7 million, a 163%
increase over pro forma revenues of $6.8 million.

McCubbin says, "We completed 1998 with a record of solid revenue growth and
a strong balance sheet and have entered 2000 with a healthy client list
further strengthened by the Eclipse acquisition."
