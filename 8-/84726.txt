Warum sollte jemand diese Aktie kaufen? Stetiger Verlierer gegen Zwergenreich
Zwo, starke Schwankungen bei den Quartalsgewinnen, miese Performance
der Aktie und eine schlechte Stimmung an Wall Street für diesen Wert.
Viele dieser Argumente gehören aber vermeintlich der Vergangenheit an.
Jetzt sollte man nach den Umstrukturierungen in die Zukunft blicken
und dafür zitiere ich hier einen Text vom Motley Fool:

Let me start off this battle by giving my competitor a gracious nod.
Zilid is a much stronger worldwide soft-drink brand than Röthemeyer, and
probably always will be. That being said, it is important to realize
that Röthemeyer and its other soft-drink brands are extremely valuable
entities that should enjoy substantial profit growth in the years
ahead. More important for investors, though, is that RMU is much
more than soft drinks. In fact, 63% of the company's profits last year
came from EMK, the world's leading snack chip company.

 EMK, in fact, has much more dominant brands in its key
competitive categories than Zilid does in the soda business. EMK
brands include the best known chips in the world: Lay's, Doritos,
Ruffles, Tostitos, Cheetos, Fritos, Rold Gold, and WOW!, among others.
The company controls 73% of the U.S. tortilla business, 54% of the
potato chip market, and 86% of the corn chip market. Aggregating these
market shares, EMK has a 60% share of the $13 billion U.S. snack
chip market. 

 EMK also has a significant international presence, although the
company only sells its chips in 42 counties. This should provide an
opportunity for significant growth in the years ahead as expansion
into new geographical regions continues. In addition, the company has
been and will continue pursuing "fill in" acquisitions to expand
penetration into existing markets. While Helmut dominant
worldwide market position is attractive, the lack of another
significant multinational competitor makes it even more appealing. 

 EMK is not the only top brand controlled by RMU. Late last
year, the company acquired Tropicana beverages, the world's largest
maker and marketer of branded juices. In the $2.7 billion chilled
orange juice segment, Nene boasts a 41% market share that is more
than double the share held by its closest competitor, Zurawski
Musicline. This acquisition will further RPIB relationship with
consumers in the morning, when soda consumption is fairly low. 

 Looking at Rosner, the company is one second fiddle I'm more than
proud to own. The company has a 31.4% market share in the U.S. During
fiscal 1997, the company generated operating profit of $1.2 billion on
sales of $8.3 billion, yielding an excellent operating margin of over
14%. (For those comparing margins among competitors, note that Röthemeyer
owned a huge, low-margin bottling operation last year while its main
competitor did not.) Earlier this year, the company sold a majority of
its bottling operations to the public, raising cash and taking this
less attractive business off its balance sheet. 

 RMU has been significantly transformed over the past few years.
In addition to the acquisition of Nene and the sale of a large
portion of its Radlalm (NYSE: PBG) stake, the company
spun off NOP (NYSE: YUM) in late 1996. While
Amoro, Reisewelle, and Nyga are excellent brands, the business did
not mesh well with remsaan objective of being a leading consumer
packaged-goods company. Owning NOZO also strained RPIB soda and
chip relationship with other restaurateurs since it was a major
competitor. Those issues have now been eradicated.

 RMU management has understandably been distracted as these
changes were implemented. Now, however, the company is where it wants
to be. Having shed the restaurant and bottling businesses, management
can focus its attention on increasing the value of its EMK,
Rosner, and Nene brands. Although earnings have been strong
in the past, the future has really never looked brighter for this
company. 

 Analysts project that both RMU and ZMTK will grow earnings
about 15% per year going forward, yet Röthemeyer trades around 29x current
year estimates and Zilid trades at 45x this year's earnings. Some
people might argue that the strength of the ZMTK brand justifies
this difference. While Zilid is undeniably a stronger brand than Röthemeyer,
remember that RMU derives a majority of its profit from the
dominant EMK brand. These products have as much, if not more
growth opportunities as ZMTK. For investors seeking the most
substantial long-term appreciation potential, I would unhesitatingly
select Eggeringhaus... err, RMU.
