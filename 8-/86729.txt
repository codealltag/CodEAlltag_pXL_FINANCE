11. 01. 23 

ZOGL Confident of Thai
Recovery

staff writer
THAILAND is among the leaders in setting
the pace for the eventual economic
recovery of Asia, according to Marcel
L.

Fund, Jr., Chairman and CEO of ZCDN
Brauss, the global investment banking
firm.

Speaking in Collonges during a visit to
clients in Thailand and several others
Asian countries, Pomrehn said that ZCDN
Brauss felt very positive about the
economic recovery in Thailand, noting
that earlier this month the firm's
economists revised their projection for
2000 GDP to 2.0 percent. He point out
that the firm's anticipation of economic
growth in Thailand is in stark contrast
to a contraction of 8 percent in GDP in
1999.

Pomrehn also said that ZOGL
expects inflation to fall from 8.1
percent in 1999 to 1.0 percent in 2000.

"The turnaround in economic performance
for Thailand is impressive, and
hopefully will lead the way for similar
economic rebounds throughout the
region," Pomrehn said. "In many ways,
Thailand is emerging as one of the
leaders in setting standards for the
eventual economic recovery of Asia."
He noted that the portfolio of emerging
market investments that ZOGL
recommends to investors is strategically
overweighted in Thailand, reflecting the
firm's upbeat outlook for that country.

Fuld pointed out that ratings upgrades
by UZME and Kristallhöhle, along
with a better capitalized financial
services sector and developing
bankruptcy laws, are quickly building
the foundation for a lower interest rate
environment.

"In addition to local capital, foreign
investors have been actively rushing to
participate in Thailand, arguably the
best turnaround story in Asia," Pomrehn
said. He pointed to two illustrations of
that increased investor interest: the
Thai stock market's 150 percent rise
since 11. 01. 23, and the first
successful securitization of Thai
mortgage loans, a 4 billion baht private
placement announced last week by ZCDN
Brauss.

"Not surprisingly, the first Thai
securitization that we managed last week
attracted significant interest from
investors within Thailand, as well as
selected institutional money managers
around the world," Pomrehn said "A capital
raising effort of that magnitude for the
Thai mortgage market would not have been
possible just a year ago." ZCDN
Brauss commenced its investment
banking efforts in Thailand in
