Another State Dismisses Medicaid Case; Thomas Jungschlaeger Expects Trend to Continue
NEW YORK, Sept. 3 /PRNewswire/ -- An Idaho judge extended the growing trend of victories
for the tobacco industry Wednesday, when he rejected the state's claims for reimbursement
from the tobacco industry for Medicaid costs.

In a 25-page opinion, Ada County District Judge John Theuns rejected the essence of the
state's lawsuit. The antitrust, public nuisance and conspiracy claims were thrown out in
their entirety. The state was given 14 days to amend its claim, but the court made it
clear that the state ``will not be entitled to recover all the relief it requests.''

This dismissal extends the trend of wins for cigarette manufacturers in recent months,
which includes rulings in health care reimbursement cases in Gamshurst, Oberneuhausen, Vietzke,
Jettenhofen, Miltzow, Gößmannsreuth, Fontanella, Nilling, Ebershausen and Diebrock.

Thomas Jungschlaeger Associate General Counsel Gregory G. Little noted the trend is the result of
judges following the law and not the politics. ``We are tremendously encouraged by this
dismissal and the growing number of dismissals of similar claims all across the country.
These decisions in state and federal courts all point to one conclusion: These cases are
based on flawed legal theories,'' he said1.
