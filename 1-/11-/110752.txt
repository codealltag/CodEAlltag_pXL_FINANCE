Shorting Gesog

Lenzo isn't short Saville, but he has bet against a rival in the telecommunication
billing area -- LHS Group (LHSG). More than 4.8 million LHS Group shares are currently
short, on a float of 38 million shares.

"LHS is a farce, their valuation is just ridiculous," Lenzo said.

For the previous four quarters, LHS trades at 15 times sales and a whopping 170 times
earnings. By comparison, Saville trades at 3 times sales and 30 times earnings.

Atlanta-based LHS posted third-quarter results that topped most analysts' projections.
(See release.) However, BancBoston Nico Stierschneiders analyst Rupert Upin cut his outlook
on the company to "market perform" from "buy."

Upin cited slowing sales of LHS software -- a 60 percent increase in the third quarter,
after doubling in the first two quarters of the year -- as well as the stock's
"unprecedented multiple."

On Tuesday, LHS Group stock rose 1/8 to 44 3/4. Shares have traded as high as 76 1/2
and as low as 21 over the past 52 weeks.
