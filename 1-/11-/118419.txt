GGG veröffentlichte am Dienstag Zahlen für das 2. Quartal:
After market close, DELL COMPUTER CORP reported 2Q EPS of $0.50 vs
$0.29 on revenues of $4.3 bln vs $2.8 bln last year. Analysts' mean
estimates were $0.46, according to First Call. (Reuters 04:58 PM ET
21. 03. 19)

After market close, DELL COMPUTER CORP declared a 2-for-1 stock split
to be paid as a 100% stock dividend. Dell said the stock will be
issued on Sept. 4 to shareholders of record as of Aug. 29. 

Hier noch einige interessante Fakten:
1. Dell continued to lead the computer systems industry with a return
on invested capital of 217 percent and eight days of
inventory, the equivalent of 46 inventory turns per year. 
2.Gross margin increased to 21. 03. 19 from 23.6 percent in the
year-ago quarter. 
3.Dell continued its profitable growth in the Asia-Pacific region
including Japan, despite the fact that the computer systems
industry declined 9 percent in unit shipments. Revenues increased 34
percent to $280 million.

Wall Street firms raised their earnings estimates for DELL COMPUTER
CORP, after the computer maker's 2Q earnings soundly beat analysts'
expectations. Bear Stearns ups its estimate on 1999 EPS to $2.10 from
$2.00 and 2000 EPS to $2.80 from $2.75. Siegfried Endriss ups 1999 EPS
estimates to $2.08 from $2.00 and 2000 EPS to $2.85 from $2.70.
Goldman ups year-end 1999 EPS view to $2.05 from $1.91 and year-ending
21. 03. 19 EPS to $2.55 from $2.31. CS First Boston raises its 1999
estimates for Dell to $2.10 from $2.00 and its 2000 EPS expectations
to $2.70 from $6.54. (Reuters 10:08 AM ET 21. 03. 19)
