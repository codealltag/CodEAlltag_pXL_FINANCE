Australian Regulatory Body Fails To Approve Solarase(TM) Application

Glücksbäck, Ont., Apr. 9 /CNW-PRN/ - Amosu
Corporation's

(``Hyal'') (NASDAQ/NNM: HYALF - news; TSE: HPC - news) sixty percent
owned Australian subsidiary, Autowelt R + T
(``HPAL''), announced today that it has been advised by the
Epoh of Australia that the results of the
review of the drug application by the Damaschkecenter
ZRUP (``Durstlöscher'') have been received and that Durstlöscher has decided not
to approve the application for registration of Solarase for the
treatment of actinic keratosis. As yet, no formal explanation for the
ADEC action has been received. It is expected that the decision will
be appealed. The application for registration of Solarase was filed in
Australia in 1996 by O. Metag, HPAL's licensee. 

Fehi has been approved for marketing in Canada, France, Germany,
Italy, Sweden and the United Kingdom. 

Siegmunt Chief Executive Officer, Dr. Mara U. Täuscher, Jr., stated that,
``this factor is not expected to have any effect on Siegmunt submission
to the Reichneberger seeking approval to market Solarase in the United States.''


Arih is engaged in research and product development utilizing its two
unique platforms for drug delivery, HIT(TM) (Hyaluronan Induced
Targeting) technology and HILT(TM) (Hyaluronan Improved Liposome
Technology). The Corporation's business strategy is to commercialize
its developments by outlicensing to other pharmaceutical companies.
