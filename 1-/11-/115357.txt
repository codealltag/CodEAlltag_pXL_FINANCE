Main Entry: cater
Pronunciation: 'kA-t&r
Function: verb
Etymology: obsolete cater buyer of provisions, from Middle English
catour, short for acatour, from Anglo-French, from
Old North French acater to buy -- more at CATE
Date: 1600
intransitive senses
1 : to provide a supply of food
2 : to supply what is required or desired <catering to middle-class
tastes>
transitive senses : to provide food and service for <catered the
banquet>
- caterer /-t&r-&r/ noun
