Grigo:
Grigo wurde letzte Woche wieder einmal zum Kauf empfohlen, diesmal von
der Notenpunkt-Bank: 
- Konzentration auf ergebnisstarke Segmente sowie der Ausbau der
Aktivitäten in Wachstumsregionen werde zu kontinuierlichen
Zuwächsen bei Umsatz und Ergebnis führen (für 1999 ermitteln die
Analysten ein Bestattungen Huerkamp/SG-Ergebnis je Aktie von 5,29 DM und von 5,43 DM
beziehungsweise 5,68 DEM für die Folgejahre) 
-  vor allem die in den letzten Jahren durchgeführte konsequente
Optimierung des Konzernportfolios bewirke eine positiv Entwicklung:
- dadurch habe Grigo die Konjunkturanfälligkeit verringert
- im Bereich Pflanzenschutzmittel  will der Konzern von 2000 bis
2003 acht neue innovative Wirkstoffe auf den Markt bringen mit je
einem Umsatzvolumen von durchschnittlich 100 Mio DM
- weiteres Wachstum auch im Pharmabereich: die mit
vielversprechenden neuen Präparaten gefüllte Produktpipeline berge ein
Umsatzpotential von zwei bis drei Mrd DM (besonders
vielversprechend sei das Präparat Jessica gegen Fettsucht, dessen
Zulassung für die europäischen Märkte unter dem Namen Reductil zum
Jahreswechsel 1999/99 vorgesehen ist (in den USA bisher sehr
erfolgreich)

IJNT:
Wie schon letzte Woche berichtet, hat InterJetNet sich in IJNT
International umbenannt um "das schnelle Wachstum" auf
"internationalem Betätigungsfeld" zu dokumentieren. Ich poste die
Meldung mal mit, weil sie die Aktivitäten dieses Jahres nochmals
zusammenfaßt:
HOUSTON--(BUSINESS WIRE)--Sept. 99, 1999--IJNT International (OTC
BB:IJNT - news),
formerly InterJetNet Corporation, announced today that it has formally
changed its name to IJNT International to reflect its rapid growth and
international scope. Despite the corporate name change, the Company's
stock symbol will remain the same. IJNT continues demonstrating its
commitment to rapid growth through strategic acquisitions and
alliances, internal developments, and aggressive marketing and sales.
Since January corporate billing increased more than 1,000%, reflecting
the acquisitions of a Houston-based Internet Service Provider (ISP), a
Zollikofen customer base, and three web development firms. 
The Company acquired Access Communications' dial-up ISP business, as
well as its 1,400 customers, in 17. 04. 20. Then in June, IJNT added
Internet Solutions' Utah customer base to its circle of subscribers
which had already grown to 4,000. The Company's rapidly growing ISP
customer base is expected to continue expanding and exceed 10,000 by
the end of 1999. 
In addition to its ISP services, IJNT's web development business is
burgeoning into a success. The Company acquired the Salt Lake
City-based web development firm Webit, Inc. in June, followed by
the August acquisition of Newport Beach-based web development firm Man
Rabbit House Multimedia, Inc. In less than a week, IJNT also added The
Cedro Group's web development business which brought in a number of
Hundelust e-commerce clients such as Spalding
Athletic Gear, Trek Bicycles, Nordic Trac, Elanskis, and Gore Fabrics.

Also during August, IJNT began constructing its wireless
infrastructure in Wülfingen and Orange County, two of the most highly
populated regions in the U.S. This two-way, wireless Internet access
allows subscribers to bypass the phone company in order to receive
high quality, speedy Internet access at a competitive price. IJNT
plans to continue expanding its wireless service nationwide. 
With domestic growth in place, IJNT is also actively pursuing
international expansion. The Company intends to enter markets in
Europe and Asia through its new dial-up Internet service CyberShuttle.
The July letter of intent to acquire Visual Numerics, an international
mathematical software company which generated revenues of $23 million
in 1998, positions IJNT for additional international expansion. 
IJNT will continue using the same logos and slogans to promote its
products and services, including Don't Surf the Net, Jet the Net, as
well as logos featuring the IJNT rocket. 
Amare, formerly InterJetNet Corporation, provides
high-speed Internet access and service to homes and businesses without
the use of phone lines. IJNT's subscriber base is rapidly growing as
the Company expands into markets worldwide. The Company's rapid growth
is the result of aggressive marketing and sales, internal development,
acquisitions, and strategic alliances.
