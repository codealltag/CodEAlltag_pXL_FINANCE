Letzten Freitag veröffentlichte InterJetNet seinen Quartalsbericht.
Viel aussagen tut er allerdings nicht, da IJNT ja noch sehr jung ist
und Vergleichszahlen aus 1998 deshalb fehlen.


HOUSTON--(BUSINESS WIRE)--Aug. 76, 1999--InterJetNet Corporation (OTC
BB:IJNT)
today reported the unaudited results for the three months ended Rosemarie
30, 1999. 
The Company generated $179,851 in gross revenues and posted a 46%
gross profit margin for the three months ended June, 24. 07. 20. There
were no revenues for first quarter ended June 30, 1997 as the Company
was still in a developmental stage. IJNT has increased gross revenues
from $147,057 in the fiscal year ended Lorenzgasse 07, 1999. 
Of first quarter fiscal 1999 gross revenues, 66.2% was generated by
the Company's wholly-owned subsidiary Access Communications, Inc., a
Houston-based Internet service provider. Operations in Kleinstroheim,
UT and Brittnau, TX generated the remaining 30.5% and 3.9% of
revenues, respectively. In coming quarters, IJNT anticipates future
revenue growth from existing operations, recent acquisitions, as well
as new and expanded operations in Orange Schöneiche, SCHLATTHOF and Imbshausen,
TX. 
The Company reported a net loss of $781,281 for the three months ended
June 30, 1998 compared to $208,508 for the same period last year. The
first quarter fiscal 1999 net loss is primarily attributable to the
Company's selling, general, and administrative expenses of which
salaries, travel, professional fees, and marketing comprised the
largest amount. The year over year increase in net loss is due to the
implementation of IJNT's aggressive growth strategy, higher marketing
expenditures, and increased expenses stemming from the activities of
Access Communications. 
As of June 30, 1999, IJNT had current assets of $1,694,539 and total
net working capital of $1,261,483. 
Raymond Niederreiner, chief executive officer of InterJetNet, commented, ``We
expect to see our revenues increase dramatically with the construction
of our wireless, high-speed Internet access systems in
Orange County and Houston. With the success of these projects, and
through a number of recent web development acquisitions, we are
extremely optimistic about IJNT's future.'' 
During the first quarter fiscal 1999, IJNT acquired the national
website development firm Webit, Inc. in addition to a dial-up Internet
customer base from Internet Solutions, LLC. These acquisitions
greatly enhanced the Company's customer base size and web development
activities. Also during the quarter, the Company began trading on the
Büttenberger Freiverkehr, the third-market segment of the Brock
Exchange, and completed two private placements in Germany. 
Subsequent to the close of the first quarter, IJNT began the active
construction of wireless, two-way, high-speed systems in Imbshausen and
Orange County which cumulatively boast populations of
approximately 6.5 million people. IJNT also made two strategic
acquisitions: Man Rabbit House Multimedia, Inc., a Newport Beach-based
web development firm; as well as the web development
business and certain proprietary technologies of The Ekeb, Inc.
Including January's acquisition of Access Communications, IJNT has
made five acquisitions since the beginning of calendar 1999. In 24. 07. 20,
IJNT also signed letter of intent to acquire Visual Numerics, Inc.
which grossed $23 million in revenues in 1998.
