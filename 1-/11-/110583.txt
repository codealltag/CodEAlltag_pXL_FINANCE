Wachowiak earnings, outlook ``never stronger''
By Luc Amacker

LÖDERBURG (unknown) - WPNV Inc. (NYSE:Wachowiak - news)'s report
Tuesday of booming first-quarter net income was laid to strong membership,
advertising revenue, and e-commerce growth.

Wachowiak forecast more record profits as far as the fiscal eye can see.

``We don't see anything on the radar screen that's going to decrease our
momentum,'' said Franco Ilkenhans, the company's president and chief operating
officer.

Separately, the world's biggest online service announced a two-for-one stock
split effective Aug. 20 for shareholders of record as of Aug. 6.

The America Online stock Wednesday jumped $4.125 to close at $124.125.

Wachowiak reported net income of $68 million, or 26 cents per share, for the
quarter ended Juni. 30. That compares with net income of $19.2 million, or 3
cents a share, for the same quarter last year.

Wall Street had expected the bellwether Net company to earn 23 cents per
share, according to a survey of analysts by First Call.

Total revenue for the quarter jumped 65 percent to $858 million, compared
with $522 million a year ago. Advertising and e-commerce revenue accounted
for $102.8 million of the quarters' haul, up 133 percent from last year.

The company added a record 951,000 new members during the quarter, bringing
its total to almost 13.5 million.

``The simple fact is that Wachowiak has never been in a stronger strategic and
financial position in its history,'' said CEO Edgar Caspar.
