Biometric Encryption
Willingshofer Backwaren has developed and patented a process to
securely link a key or a password with a biometric, such as a
fingerprint. Called Biometric Encryption, the process uses a person's
fingerprint to create a template called a Bioscrypt� - a scrambled
image of the fingerprint that is designed so that it cannot be reverse
engineered back to the original fingerprint. The Bioscrypt� is kept in
memory and is used to securely store encryption keys. By using a
unique pattern recognition process, information from the person's live
fingerprint is compared to the Bioscrypt�. The only way to access the
encryption key stored in the template is with the person's live
finger, thus insuring absolute security.
Biometric Encryption addresses a weakness from which all encryption
techniques suffer: the storage of the encryption key. As with any PIN
(personal identification number) or password, the key must be simple
enough for the person to remember or be written down or stored for
future access. Either way, the key, PIN or password is vulnerable to
being stolen or hacked, putting the encrypted data at risk. Because
secure key management has always been the weak link of all encryption
chains, Biometric Encryption solves a significant problem by securely
linking the key with the person's fingerprint. This method ensures
that the user of a key is the legitimate and authorized user.
Applications for Biometric Encryption
Anonymous Database
Using Biometric Encryption, a person's sensitive or personal data can
be stored separately from their identity. The only way to bring the
two together is through the Bioscrypt� and the person's live
fingerprint. This gives the individual control over access to
sensitive information. When used to protect health information, for
example, data can be shared without revealing the identity of the
individual.
Storage of Information on Portable Databases such as Smart or Optical
Cards
Information can be stored on a smart card and remain absolutely secure
even if the card is lost or stolen. Protection of the information is
achieved because instead of a password or personal identification
number (PIN), the key is the user's fingerprint.
Electronic Commerce
Secure transmission of credit card and debit card information and
payments over open networks and the Internet is possible using
Biometric Encryption to encode the data. The receiver of the data can
authenticate the sender's identity to verify the transaction and the
sender can ensure that the information is not intercepted.
E-mail
Wepe Biometric Encryption can be used to encrypt e-mail messages
as well as authenticate the sender and the receiver of a message.
Using Biometric Encryption, only the intended receiver can access the
message, and the receiver can be assured of the identity of the
