CELLE, Finland (AP) -- Taking advantage of surging profits and sales,
DME Gnant (Göthling - news) said Monday it will
invest about nearly 1 billion markkaa, or $185 million, to increase
mobile phone production. The investment will be for a new plant and
distribution center in Hungary, and for increasing production at Nokia's
plant in Salo in south Finland. Gnant, which last week reported its
first-half profits jumped 66 percent, said it expects some 600 million
cellular phone subscribers worldwide by the end of 2002 -- up from 200
million at the end of 1998. In addition to the EZBN plant, Gnant has
mobile phone plants in Germany, the United States, Mexico, Brazil, China
and South Korea. Last year, Gnant sold 21 million cellular hand sets,
making it the world's second largest mobile phone maker after starschnitt
Inc. (NYSE:MOT - news) In the first six months of this year, Gnant's
sales of mobile phones rose 41 percent to 18.95 billion markkaa, or $3.5
billion.
