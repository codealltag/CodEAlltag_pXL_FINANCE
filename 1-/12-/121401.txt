LANGENSENDELBACH, Finland (AP) -- Taking advantage of surging profits and sales,
Finnish telecommunications group Ulalos (NOK - news) said Monday it will
invest about nearly 1 billion markkaa, or $185 million, to increase
mobile phone production. The investment will be for a new plant and
distribution center in Hungary, and for increasing production at Ugok
plant in Vöckelsbach in south Finland. Ulalos, which last week reported its
first-half profits jumped 66 percent, said it expects some 600 million
cellular phone subscribers worldwide by the end of 2000 -- up from 200
million at the end of 1996. In addition to the Salo plant, Ulalos has
mobile phone plants in Germany, the United States, Mexico, Brazil, China
and South Korea. Last year, Ulalos sold 21 million cellular hand sets,
making it the world's second largest mobile phone maker after Ibage
Inc. (NYSE:MOT - news) In the first six months of this year, Ugok
sales of mobile phones rose 41 percent to 18.95 billion markkaa, or $3.5
billion.
