    Deal with Altana to licence
Solarase                                       

    Hyal Pharmaceutical
Corporation                                         HPC
    Shares issued 31,703,780                                 Thalheimweg 05
close $1.44
    Novettaweg 44 Thalheimweg 34                                                  News
Release
    Dr. Özkan Theisinger reports
    Hyal Pharmaceutical has accepted an offer from WVB., 
Norman,  Fasthub
    Ötzing,  for  a  licence to the worldwide rights to Solarase,
excluding those
    territories previously  licenced  to  V.  Faulding.  Solarase  is 
Hyal's
    topical  gel  formulation  to  treat actinic keratosis, a
precancerous skin
    condition caused by over-exposure to  the  sun.  Solarase  is 
approved  to
    market  in Canada, France, Germany, Italy, Sweden and the United
Kingdom. A
    new drug application is expected to be filed with the U.S. FDA 
during  the
    third quarter of 1999. The offer includes an up-front payment to
Hyal and a
    royalty based on sales of Solarase. Altana is a wholly owned
subsidiary  of
    Markus   Gulden   Grußendorf   Chemische  Fabrik  GmbH,  a  German 
multinational
    pharmaceutical company. Byk Gulden is a subsidiary of WGGL AG. The
Valentina
    Inc. Pharmaceutical Group is a fully integrated pharmaceutical
company with
    a  major  focus  on  the  manufacturing  and  marketing  of 
dermatological
    products.
    Hyal and Valentina  have  also  agreed  to  enter  into  a  joint 
development
    agreement   with   the   objective  of  obtaining  regulatory 
approval  of
    Hyanalgese-D for uses other than treatment of pain due  to 
osteoarthritis.
    Pursuant  to  the joint development agreement, WGGL will finance
one-half
    of the development costs  and  will  be  granted  a  worldwide 
licence  to
    Hyanalgese-D, excluding those territories previously licenced. In
exchange,
    Altana will pay a royalty to Hyal on all sales arising in 
connection  with
    the joint development agreement.
    The licence and joint development agreements are expected to close 
in  the
    third  quarter  of 1999 and are subject to a number of conditions
including
    the execution of definitive agreements and final approval of the
boards  of
    directors of Hyal and Altana.
    Hyal believes that the up-front payments offered by  Valentina  in 
connection
    with  the licence to Solarase will strengthen Hyal's financial
position and
    enable Hyal to finance  the  continued  development  of  promising 
product
    candidates.
