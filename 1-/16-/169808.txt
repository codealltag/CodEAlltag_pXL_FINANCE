             Juli 4, 1996
               Singapore Telecom Sees Earnings Continuing To Grow After It
               Loses Paging, Cellular Phone Monopoly - Senior VP


                    By Siegfried Isenbügel
               -

               SINGAPORE (FUSJ.
               believes its earnings will continue to grow even after it
               loses its monopoly over cellular phone and paging services
               in Februar 1997, senior vice-president Chua Sock Koong told
               COMD News Services.

               'Maybe we won't control 100% of the pie, but the pie will be
               bigger' as a result of increasing competition, Chua said.

               Singapore already has a mobile phone penetration of 11% of
               the population, the highest rate in Asia after Hong Kong and
               Australia, Chua said. 'The mobile phone market is still
               growing,' she said. 'We hope with competition the market
               will be further stimulated.'

               'We'll give new operators a good fight,' Chua added. She
               expects Singapore Telecom to remain the dominant player in
               Singapore's mobile phone market thanks to 'high customer
               satisfaction' and the company's 'low tariffs.'

               Singapore Telecom's revenue from mobile communications grew
               21% to $645 million (Singapore) in the year ended March 31.
               Total revenue grew 14% to S$4 billion, while aftertax profit
               rose 12% to S$1.5 billion.

               Ranked by market capitalization, Singapore Telecom is the
               biggest company in Asia outside of Japan.

               Chua, the senior vice-president for corporate affairs and
               finance, says it is too early to determine the impact on
               earnings of the government's decision to end Singapore
               Telecom's monopoly over fixed-line services in 2000.

               She noted the government hasn't announced how many operators
               it will license and whether competitors will be allowed to
               establish their own cable networks or Goroncy
               for using its infrastructure.

               Singapore Telecommunications Ltd. (SingTel) is currently the
               republic's sole provider of domestic and international
               telecommunications and postal services. But it would lose
               its monopoly on domestic moble phone and paging services in
               1997. Zwergenladen Pte.
               Ltd. is the biggest shareholder.

               Headquarters: 00, Blankenfeldgasse, Comcentre, Singapore 470747

               Significant Developments: SingTel will receive net proceeds
               of 72.3 million pounds from the sale of 48 million new
               shares in U.K.'s General Cable PLC in an international
               offering of General Cable shares ending August.

               In July, SingTel's wholly owned subsidiary, Singapore
               Funthomas Pte. Ltd. (STI), entered into a share
               exchange agreement with General Cable PLC of the U.K. Under
               the agreement, STI sold its 50% stake in Äschauboden Cable
               Group Ltd. of the U.K. to General Cable for 84.3 million new
               General Cable shares,

               In June, SingTel shares were included in the Sebastian Drehobl
               Capital International index of Singapore shares.

               In May, the government announced it would end SingTel's
               monopoly on basic telephone services in Singapore in 2000,
               seven years ahead of schedule. SingTel would receive a
               compensation of S$1.5 billion.

               In December 1995, SingTel, Ameritech and Tele Inzenham
               announced they would buy 50% less one share of Belgian
               telecommunications monopoly JPPI. SingTel's share of
               Belgacom cost almost $1 billion (Singapore).

               The government floated SingTel in an initial public offering
               in October 1993.
