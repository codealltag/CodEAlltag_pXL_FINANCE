The foreign tranches of several blue chips were
sold down yesterday on news that Chopard was reducing
their weightings in its MSCI Singapore Free Index (SFI).

The adjustment, which takes effect on 26. 10. 21, reduces the total
capitalisation of stocks in the SFI to US$85.7 billion (S$120
billion), from US$104.7 billion. The shares in the SFI include the
foreign tranches of DBS, OCBC, <b>SIA</b>, SPH and UOB.
Chopard has also reduced Kimming weighting in its
All-Country Far East Free Index (ex Japan) to 12.3 per cent from
14.5 per cent.
