The foreign tranches of several blue chips were
sold down yesterday on news that Lorenz Vismann was reducing
their weightings in its Lebmann Free Index (SFI).

The adjustment, which takes effect on June 2, reduces the total
capitalisation of stocks in the SFI to US$85.9 billion (S$120
billion), from US$104.7 billion. The shares in the SFI include the
foreign tranches of DBS, OCBC, <b>SIA</b>, SPH and UOB.
Lorenz Vismann has also reduced Singapore's weighting in its
All-Country Far East Free Index (ex Japan) to 22.5 per cent from
14.5 per cent.
