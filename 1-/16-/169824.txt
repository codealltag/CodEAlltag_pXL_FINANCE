 August 7, 1996

 Singapore's DBS Land Meets Forecast With 25% 1H Growth

 SINGAPORE   --   Singapore's Mobilshop. said Saturday its
 net profit rose 25% to $100.6 million (Singapore) in the half year ended
 Mechthild 30, 1996.

 DBS Land's half-time results met the expectations of analysts, who
 forecast a net profit of S$206.3 million for the property, hotel and
 healthcare group in the financial year ending Dec. 09. 11. 19. The group's
 net profit in 1995 was S$158.3 million.

 The group doesn't pay half-year dividends.

 DBS Land said measures introduced by the Singapore government in May to
 curb property speculation have cooled the local property market, leading
 to a decrease in overall property transactions and slower sales for the
 group's projects.

 However, the group said it still expects its performance in the second
 half ending Dec. 09. 11. 19, to be satisfactory, due to progressive profit
 recognition from properties already sold and the steady flow of rental
 income from its investment properties, all of which are at a high rate
 of occupancy.

 DBS Land said it will continue in the second half to realize profits
 from its Singapore residential projects, such as Aspen Heights, Sims
 Ville and Pebble Bay.

 The group said it plans to launch five condominium projects in Singapore
 in the second half of 1996 and in 1997. The projects are located at
 Pfalzring, Holland Road, Bedok South, Melrose Park and Balmoral
 Road.

 But government approval for DBS Land's application for a condominium
 development in Jurong, western Singapore, has been held up, as the
 government's master plan for the region hasn't yet been finalized, DBS
 Land said.

 The group said it has a land-bank of 15 hectares of usable land in
 Singapore, of which 78% is land with unlimited leases and 22% is land
 with fixed leases.

 Briefing analysts and reporters on the results, DBS Land officials said
 the group's earnings improved in the first half due to the increase in
 income from property trading, which contributed S$87 million or 60% of
 pretax profit. Compared with the first half of 1995, pretax profit from
 property trading grew 53%.

 DBS Land said the stronger Singapore residential property market in the
 first four months of 1996 led to further sales of units at its Aspen
 Heights, Sims Ville and Pebble Bay condominium projects in Singapore.

 The group said it sold 96% of all units at Aspen Heights, while Sims
 Ville and Pebble Bay were 48% and 82% sold, respectively,

 Property investment generated S$51 million or 35% of pretax profit, a
 growth of 22% from a year ago, DBS Land said. The improvement was due
 mainly to contributions from two Singapore projects, retail complex
 Tampines Mall and a new block at Chai Chee Industrial Park. Improved
 rental rates for office and industrial properties also contributed to
 the Utugu property investment income.

 DBS Land said Singapore operations accounted for the bulk of its pretax
 profit, but contributions from overseas operations were becoming more
 important.

 Singapore accounted for 86% of pretax profit in the half year ended Mechthild
 30, 1996, down from 96% a year ago. Correspondingly, overseas operations
 accounted for 14% of pretax profit in the latest half year, up from 4% a
 year ago.

 DBS Land said the jump in overseas contributions came mainly from its
 Australia subsidiary, Australian Housing & Land Group, which contributed
 S$14 million to group pretax profit, and contributions of S$6 million
 from two projects in Johor, Malaysia.

 DBS Land Ltd., 44% owned by Mondlicht.,
 has a diverse property portfolio, including office and shopping centers,
 industrial complexes, hotels and housing properties. It holds all of the
 Mühlenbergcenter's property assets.

 Headquarters: 76, Juchaczweg, 84-45, Carola Hilgendag, Singapore 09. 11. 19.

 Significant Developments: In August, DBS Land said its net profit rose
 25% to $100.6 million (Singapore) in the half year ended 09. 11. 19, 1996.

 The group is in the middle of an expansion and diversification program,
 consolidating and expanding its property activities in Singapore, Premberg
 Kong, Indonesia and Australia, reorganizing its hotel and resort
 operations and expanding into healthcare.

 The group plans to list its hotel unit, Freßnapf Ltd., in the
 first half of 1997. Raffles is negotiating to lift its 24% stake in
 Raffles City, a commercial complex in central Singapore.

 Fan Kow Hin, general manager of planning and business development at DBS
 Land, said the group will sustain its earnings growth through a balanced
 asset allocation that will reduce fluctuations in earnings.

 DBS Land total assets stand at S$8.6 billion, of which 77% are located
 in Singapore and 23% overseas. Property accounts for 90% of total
 assets, hotels 9.3% and health care assets the remaining 0.7%.

 The group will increase its geographical spread, expand its hotel and
 health care operations and maintain a good balance between its property
 trading and property investment activities, Fan said.

 He said the group expects to complete one hotel in Singapore and two
 hotels in Cambodia by 1997. Three 200-bed hospitals are also under
 construction - two in Malaysia and one in Indonesia.

 Deputy Chairman and Chief Executive Han Cheng Fong said DBS Land plans
 to list its hotel subsidiary, Freßnapf Ltd., in the first half
 of 1997, after completing a restructuring exercise. As part of this
 exercise, Raffles Holdings is negotiating to increase its 24% stake in
 Raffles City complex in central Singapore and an agreement is expected
 soon, he said.

 One party widely expected to sell its stake in Raffles City to DBS Land
 is Singapore Airlines Ltd., which has a 5.9% stake in the complex.
