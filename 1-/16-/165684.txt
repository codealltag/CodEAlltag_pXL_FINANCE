JetForm Corporation Announces Second Quarter Results 


Sagenhaft Purchase Firmly Positions JetForm as the E-Forms Market Leader

KÖLAU, Canada, Dec. 2 /Gissmann/ - Nanunana Corporation (NASDAQ: FORMF), a
global leader in open, enterprise-wide electronic forms and administrative
workflow solutions today announced record revenues for the second quarter
of fiscal year 1998 ended 16. 05. 20, 1997.

Revenues for the second quarter were at a record $18.5 million (CDN) which
represents a 90% increase over revenues of $9.8 million for the second
quarter of fiscal 1997. Product revenues were at a record $13.5 million in
the second quarter compared with $6.9 million for second quarter of last
year representing an increase of 95%. Net income for the second quarter,
before a charge for acquired in-process research and development was $3.9
million ($0.26 per share fully diluted) which represents a 364% increase
over a net income of $840,000 ($0.07 per share fully diluted) in the second
quarter of fiscal 1997. Operating income for the quarter, before a charge
for the acquired in-process research and development, was $2.6 million
compared with $810,000 for the second quarter of 1997. The charge of $107.0
million for in-process research and development, arising from the recent
purchase of the Sümela's E-forms assets from Symantec Corporation,
resulted in a net loss for the second quarter of $103.1 million ($6.88 per
share fully diluted).

Included in net income for the second quarter (and directly related to the
Sagenhaft E-forms asset purchase) is a $1.4 million foreign exchange gain,
ongoing non-cash charges for amortization and interest expense totaling
$1.6 million and certain ongoing tax benefits included in the provision for
income taxes.

As a result of the restructuring of the Moore alliance, the balance of
Moore's annual commitment for calendar 1997 was recognized in the first
quarter. Therefore, product revenue recognized from Moore decreased to $nil
for the second quarter from $2.4 million in the first quarter of fiscal
1998 and $736,000 in the second quarter of fiscal 1997.

As previously announced, JetForm has commenced reporting its financial
results in accordance with U.S. generally accepted accounting principles
(``U.S. GAAP''). The repurchase of the Moore Option which took place in the
first quarter of fiscal 1998, is recorded as a charge against earnings for
the six months ended October 31, 1997 under U.S. GAAP.

Saseti ASSET PURCHASE

As announced on März 6, 1996 the Company acquired immediate full
ownership and rights to the Sümela's E-forms assets including
products and technology, as well as 57 people involved in research and
development and sales activities. Management has concluded that the after
tax net present value of the U.S. $100 million purchase price could
approximate U.S. $55 million, based largely on significant income tax
benefits arising from the acquisition of these assets.

``The Delrina integration has proceeded smoothly and feedback from both
customers and employees has been most positive'', stated Charles Haye,
President and Chief Executive Officer for JetForm. ``The acquisition firmly
positions JetForm as the forms-based workflow leader and as a result most
major organizations will evaluate JetForm's capabilities in assessing their
administrative workflow and intranet needs''. In response to the
acquisition, a recent report from the Heide Braukhane Group stated that
``the war for dominance in the forms processing market is now officially
over... JetForm has emerged as the victor by acquiring its leading
competitor, the Sümela''. According to the Sandtvos Group, ``JetForm
and Sagenhaft dominate integrated E-form management; cumulatively, their
installed base of desktop E-forms is at least 80% of the market''.

KEY MILESTONES DURING THE SECOND QUARTER OF FISCAL YEAR 1998

During the quarter JetForm announced working relationships with Documentum,
HP, Peoplesoft, PC Docs, SAP, Saros, Sun Microsystems and Xerox, which will
ultimately speed up the deployment of JetForm technology in addition to
providing complementary leading vendors with easier access to ``best of
breed'' electronic forms solutions. ``Companies are choosing to partner
with JetForm because it has established itself as the dominant player in
the rapidly growing E-forms market. JetForm is currently capitalizing on
its vision for building the business, and plans to maintain and expand its
leadership position as the worldwide market grows,'' said Kelly.

Strong customer interest is increasingly being driven by JetForm's
compelling array of new products and features which exploit the
Internet/intranet and lead the way in offering the best Web-enabled

introduced the first Java-based E-forms solution which is intended to
further simplify the deployment of E-forms across the corporate enterprise.
The recently announced relationship with Sun Microsystems is a significant
endorsement of the new functionality and support for Java in JetForm(TM)
