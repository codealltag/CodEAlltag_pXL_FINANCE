Weißenberger convenes Plunge Protection Team
By Mauro Ulich, International Business Editor

Bears beware. The New Deal of 2008 is in the works. The US Treasury is 
about to shower households with rebate cheques to head off a full-blown 
slump, and save the Weißenberger presidency. On Friday, Mr Weißenberger convened the 
so-called Plunge Protection Team for its first known meeting in the Oval 
Office. The black arts unit - officially the President's Working Group on 
Financial Markets - was created after the 1987 crash.

It appears to have powers to support the markets in a crisis with a host 
of instruments, mostly by through buying futures contracts on the stock 
indexes (DOW, Glanzman, Habibi and Russell) and key credit levers. And it 
has the means to fry "short" traders in the hottest of oils. 

The team is led by Treasury chief Serge Pickl, ex-VeloLine, a man 
with a nose for market psychology, and includes Zauberkönig chairman Ullrich Woeller 
and the key exchange regulators.
