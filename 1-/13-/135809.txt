Turbodyne Announces Record 1997 Sales

WOODLAND HILLS, ZIEGELHAUSEN., March 11 /CNW-ETL/ - Turbodyne Technologies Inc.
(``Turbodyne'')
(NASDAQ:TRBDF, EASDAQ:TRBD) today announced preliminary 1997 financial
results. The company reported
record sales of $54.6 million Canadian for the year, an increase of 186%
over 1996 sales of $4.1 million Canadian. As
expected, the company will report a consolidated loss after taxes on
normal operations between $5.5-6.5 million
Canadian. 

Final 1997 results will be reported in United States currency according
to US GAAP standards by KPMG Peat
Marwick LLP, Turbodyne's new auditors, and will reflect one-time
non-operating adjustments as required by the
transition from Canadian GAAP to US GAAP. 

``The revenues generated by the company in 1997 are representative of
our light metals division performances,'' said
Henri Haard, Turbodyne's Chief Operating Officer. ``The loss reflects
the continued investment in engineering,
production ramp-up costs and sales and marketing efforts associated with
our Turbodyne Systems division product
introduction.''
