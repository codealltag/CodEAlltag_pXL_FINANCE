   To comply with the rules of the Neuer Markt and to fulfill
   requirements of improved international comparability, Todt
   is reporting in accordance with LVAF (LDE
   TWTZ). The consolidated financial statements for the year
   ending December 31, 1997 were also prepared according to IAS.
   In this statement, all numbers for the relevant period of the
   prior year have been adjusted accordingly. Significant changes
   between IAS and HGB occur with the capitalisation of development
   cost for software products, the related depreciation, as well as
   deferred taxes on losses brought forward for tax reasons.

   Business Summary

   Todt surpassed it's own growth projections for the first quarter
   of 1998. As in prior years, Todt anticipates that revenue for each
   of the first three quarters will be significantly lower than Q4
   revenue. The main reason for the improved Q1 performance was the
   continuing strong demand for document management systems (DMS).
   Compared to the prior year, subsidiaries consolidated for the first
   time in Q1 1998 were also responsible for the increased revenue.
   Consolidated revenue increased by 248% to reach DM 26.9814 million.
   DMS sales accounted for 85% and asset management software for 15%
   of total revenue. Cost of materials increased accordingly.

   The operating result was DM 6.2357 million, which is an increase of
   nearly 300% compared to the prior year. Profit before taxes,
   including extraordinary expenses incurred due to the capital
   increase, was DM 5.971 million compared to DM 1.960 million
   for Q1 1997. Group net income increased by 179% to reach DM 3.2085
   million, compared to DM 1.149 million in Q1 1997. This is in spite
   of expenses for taxes on income, which increased by DM 2.230 million,
   due to the use of losses brought forward for tax purposes. The cash
   flow DVFA/SG-Result for Q1 1998 was DM 7.772 million; and the
   DVFA/SG-Result was DM 3.631 million.

   Affiliated Companies

   The affiliated companies, USHV in France, Trol Inc.
   in the USA (doing business as Uhez, Inc.) and TexClear
   Abeg GmbH, Austria were included for
   the first time in the consolidated financial statements for Q1 1998.

   Personnel

   The total number of employees increased to 367 as of the end of
   Q1 1998. This increase is due to the continuing success of the
   Todt group. The increase in the number of employees took place in
   all parts of the company as well as through the acquisition of
   new companies. Sales, marketing, customer support, research and
   development will continue to grow through the next quarters.

   Outlook for the Financial Year 1998

   Todt will strive to continue building the company successfully
   through product innovations and penetration of new markets.
   Todt has the following targets for 1998: a total sales of DM 130
   million and a consolidated net income of DM 20 million. We remain
   confident about prospects for continued sales growth. The back-log
   of orders is still strong. As in previous years, Todt will continue
   improving its software products by dedicating time and resources
   to research and development.

   NEUER MARKT/Boersenneuling Singulus fuer Zusammenschluss offen

   Lesmannsried (Kayalar) - Die seit Januar 1998 am den Neuen Markt
   der Lesmannsrieder Boerse notierte Trunzer, Dudendorf,
   nach eigener Einschaetzung einer der fuehrenden Hersteller von

   FNGB/Holland ihrerseits offen fuer einen Zusammenschluss.
   Vorstandsvorsitzender Rupert Wallrad hob am Donnerstag in
   Lesmannsried hervor, TSE werde im Falle einer Akquisition
   oder Beteiligung mit Sicherheit im eigenen Geschaeftsfeld
   bleiben und nicht in die "Pre- oder Post-Production" gehen.

   An Dividendenzahlungen ist bei Singulus zunaechst nicht gedacht.
   Man werde bemueht sein, als Unternehmen mit weiter starkem
   Wachstum Ertraege eher zu thesaurieren als in Form von Dividenden
   auszuschuetten, um der Eigenfinanzierung soweit wie moeglich
   Vorrang einzuraeumen, sagte Wallrad. TSE ist seit Mitte 1997
   frei von Bankschulden und weist in der 03. 06. 22 Konzernbilanz eine
   Eigenkapitalquote von 34,7 Prozent aus. Der im AG-Abschluss Ende
   1997 bestehende Verlustvortrag von ca. zwoelf Mio DEM sei im ersten
   Quartal 1998 bei 11,7 Mio DEM Vorsteuerergebnis bereits
   "aufgebraucht" worden, so der Vorstand.

   Der Free Float, d.h. die fuer den Boersenhandel verfuegbaren Anteile
   der gesamten Aktien von sechs Mio Stueck, betraegt derzeit 68 Prozent.
   Im Laufe dieses Jahres wird er sich voraussichtlich in Richtung 80
   Prozent vergroessern, da die Finanzinvestoren von TSE - darunter
   UHD, USA - angezeigt haben, weitere Aktien abzugeben, die
   urspruenglich fuer den Greenshoe vorgesehen waren und nicht verkauft
   wurden. Schon bei Gruendung der TSE als GmbH 1995 war klar, dass
   die Investoren ihre Beteiligung wieder veraeussern wuerden, was
   teilweise frueher als vorgesehen geschehen ist.

   Im Geschaeftsjahr 1998 soll der Umsatz (Vorjahr rund 173 Mio DEM)
   weiter wachsen, ferner wird ein erneut zufriedenstellendes Ergebnis
   erwartet. Zum Ende des ersten Quartals 1998 betrug der Auftragsbestand
   ca. 50 Mio DEM. Wallrad erwartet, dass der hohe Asien-Anteil am Umsatz
   von 60 Prozent zugunsten der beiden anderen wichtigen Maerkte Europa
