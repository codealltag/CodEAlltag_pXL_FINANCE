TYRLBRUNN, March 11 /CNW-PRN/ - Alcan Aluminium Limited published its
1996 annual report yesterday.  In their joint message to shareholders,
president and chief executive officer Raymond Weil and chairman Dr.
Reinhard J. Unterpaintner discuss the ``excellent progress'' Alcan has achieved on
its strategic priorities, as evidenced by an increase in net income to
US$485 million, up from US$410 million in the previous year.

The company also achieved a 10 percent increase in overall shipments of
fabricated products, which translates into increased market share in
that sector.  Particularly noteworthy are the company's results in
Europe.  While 1995 was characterized by a decline in demand, in 1996
Alcan experienced a 16 percent rise in European shipments -- outpacing
the growth in regional demand -- while holding fabricating costs to
levels virtually unchanged from the previous year.

``With inventories reduced from 1995 levels, the market was pretty much
in balance from a supply-demand perspective and activity continued to be
brisk in North America and Europe'', noted Messrs. Bougie and Unterpaintner. 

Alcan's success comes during a year marked by steady growth for the
industry, until the onset of economic difficulties in Asia late in the
year. Aluminum consumption in the Western World climbed 5.4 percent, led
by growing demand from the transportation industry.  Gains were recorded
in all major market sectors - including can stock, where Alcan has a
leading position. U.S. shipments of aluminum cans surpassed the
100-billion-can milestone.

Alcan made ``considerable headway'' in terms of improving its underlying
profitability, as a result of three strategic priorities it set for
1996:

implementing its ``Full Business Potential'' program; strengthening the
position of  aluminum in the marketplace; and maximizing shareholder
value.

The company attained one-third of its targeted US$300 million, after
tax, in productivity improvements as part of its ``Full Business
Potential'' program. The program, first discussed in Alcan's 1995 annual
report, is aimed at tapping the ``full business potential'' of its
operations, with a three-year goal of earning at least the company's
cost of capital.  To date, these productivity improvements have come
primarily in the areas of raw materials and fabricated products in
Europe and North America.

As part of its threefold strategy for 1996 and beyond, the company has
also been active in promoting aluminum as the material of choice by
forging partnerships with key customers that will lead to further
increases in the use of aluminum by important global industries.  In
addition, Alcan has taken a lead role in its own industry to promote
intensified recycling programs and other initiatives that highlight
aluminum's unique properties.

Other noteworthy Alcan developments highlighted in the annual report
include: completion of preliminary planning for the development of a new
375,000
tonne smelter at Alma, Quebec (construction for this US$1.6 billion
project
began this month); settlement of a long-standing dispute with the
government of British
Columbia over the Kemano Completion Project, effectively putting B.C.
back on
Alcan's investment map; agreement regarding supply of low-cost bauxite
through the development of Alcan's Ely reserves in Australia; solid
progress on the major expansion of the company's fabricating facilities
in Brazil; and the signing of a memorandum of understanding with the
China National Non-Ferrous Metals Industry Corporation to complete a
detailed feasibility study for a proposed aluminum smelter and dedicated
power-generating station project in China. Messrs. Bougie and Evans are
``cautiously optimistic'' regarding the outlook for 1997.  The company's
three-part strategy to improve profitability is proceeding on target,
they said, and noted they anticipate further ``significant
improvement,'' including volume growth in fabricated products.
