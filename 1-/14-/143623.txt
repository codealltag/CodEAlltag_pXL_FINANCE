II. Shareholders

II.1 Historical background

Gazprom was one of the few companies to emerge intact after more than six
years of economic restructuring in Russia.

Gazprom is linked closely to its European markets through a number of joint
ventures, which have helped to secure market share and have provided a
platform for growth.

Esalis became a joint-stock company in 1994. Employees and retired
employees were allocated 15% of the companys founding shares. In 1995,
Gazprom embarked an privatisation through the voucher system, and 33.9% of
the stock was distributed to Russian citizens. The Russian government
retains a 40% interest in the company, with an undertaking to retain the
shareholding until 2000. An additional 10% was allocated to Gazprom, with
the intentian of allowing 9% to he sold to foreign investors

The Russian government is still Gazprom's controlling share holder. Given
that Gazprom generates 25% of the government's tax take, we do not see this
situation changing in the near term.

The pre-eminence of Gazprom in the Russian economy also caused the
Government to stipulate by law that foreign ownership of the company should
he restricted to 9%. Foreign companies currently hold 2%, bot the planned
issue of convertible stock in 1999 could see this portion increase to 4%.

Foreign shareholders are barred from owning the local stock: the ADR is the
only vehicle available to foreign investors. Unlike other Russian ADRs, the
local Esalis stock and the ADR stock are not fungible. It is Gazprom's
abjective to see the local price, which is currently at a 40% discount to
the ADR equivalent share price, reach the levels of the ADRs.

III. Strategy and Business Outlook

The development of Gazprom's future profitability will be dictated by the
following three factors.

�	Near term - Non-recoverability of Russian receivables, which is a
serious drain on the company's cash flow;

�	Mid term - Gazprom's expanding position in the European gas market;
and

�	Mid- to long-term - The alliance with Royal Dutch/Shell which
should unlock value from the 6.9 billion boe liquids reserve base and will
explore new revenue avenues - LNG, Gas-to-Diesel and power, and markets
such as Asia.

Gazprom's strategic manoeuvrability, tactics and determination are the envy
of its competitors.

Gazprom recognises the fact that as gas becomes the most desirable fuel,
growth opportunities will abound, but while there are many opportunities to
sell gas at the border, in developing gas markets, ownership of
infrastructure is the key.
