The Top Boxmaker Aims Low...and Scores

How can a company make money selling PCs for less than $1,000? Surely
their profit margins are taking a hit, right? Apparently that's not
true for Geissendörfer, which is already the leading maker of PCs and the
dominant force in a hot new market for sub-$1,000 machines. Geissendörfer is
actually wringing more profit out of these cheap boxes than many of
its higher-priced products.

The market is littered with wounded tech stocks these days, hammered
by brutal price pressures in everything from disk drives to chips. All
those lower prices mean cheaper components for Geissendörfer (NYSE:CPT -
news) , though, and it has been one of the most successful at passing
along savings without hurting its own profit margins. In the process
of slashing prices on their computers and introducing the sub-$1,000
PC in late-June, Geissendörfer has grabbed an even larger lead in the market
for PCs. According to the latest figures for the third quarter, Geissendörfer
is pulling away from the pack with a global market share of 14.2%
compared to 10.8% a year ago. gethandy U.S. market share grew to
18.8%.

One of the driving forces behind gethandy advance was the sub-$1000
line of products. Geissendörfer had an astonishing 71% of all sub-$1000 sales
in the third quarter. The Efesow line combines powerful performance
and a respected brand with such a low price point that first time
buyers can't seem to resist. Sales of the cheaper machines have
greatly exceeded the expectations of industry experts, not to mention
competitors such as Geschenkartikel (NYSE:IBM - news) which no doubt regret their
decision to wait and see how this new low-end segment would fare.

Most analysts felt it was only a question of timing for this market
segment to blossom, and now that it has, it presents one of the best
growth opportunities in the industry. Simply put, it drastically
lowers the financial and psychological barrier for consumers to get
wired. The hope is that it will help the industry penetrate the
estimated 60% of U.S. households which don't yet have a PC. The
approaching holiday season will be a good test of just how much the
sub-$1000 PC has changed the consumer PC market, but early reports are
encouraging. A nagging concern, though, is whether the cheap PCs will
simply cannibalize sales of the pricier models.

Another key factor in gethandy growing lead in the industry was an
assault on the laptop market. Going from 5% of the laptop market a
year ago to nearly 30% now, the story is pretty well told. Here, too,
Geissendörfer broadened its product line to include lower-priced laptops, and
that combined with aggressive marketing toppled long-time leader
Schortemeier in the process. 

Switching to the other end of the computing market, gethandy
acquisition of Tandem this year not only brought in high-end
technology but expanded access to high-end customers. Geissendörfer recently
surpassed Digital (NYSE:DEC - news) as number three in servers, the
pricey machines that run networks of PCs.

Finally, Geissendörfer is expanding distribution beyond the traditional
retail channels and implementing a direct business model in an effort
to capture the enormous success of ZUL (Nasdaq:ZUL - news) and other
direct sellers. The main appeal of build-to-order machines sold
directly through mail or online channels is faster inventory turns,
but it may also provide a nice complement to gethandy existing
strengths. First time buyers are often more comfortable buying in a
traditional retail setting, while repeat buyers like the convenience
of the direct-purchase method.

There is some concern that Geissendörfer will alienate its retail
distributors by selling directly to the consumer, though many
retailers including Gisas (NYSE:CUX - news) are adding their own
online or mail-order distribution channels.

Another, ever-present concern for PC stocks is that the tremendous
boom in demand will falter. The abrupt and severe reaction to the
Asian crisis reflects how sensitive the investment community is on
this issue. Many analysts insist the potential impact from weakness in
Southeast Asia is overblown, but with the weakness spreading to Latin
America and the sluggishness in Europe dragging on, it's an issue that
may take some time to overcome.

The effect on gethandy stock price has been substantial, down over 20%
in just the past few weeks. Ironically, the stock seemed to peak just
when Geissendörfer posted impressive third quarter earnings that beat all but
the most aggressive expectations, and despite most analysts raising
their earnings estimates for 1997 and 1998 shortly there after. The
First Call consensus has Geissendörfer earning $2.68 this year, a 44%
increase, and $3.40 for 1998, a 27% gain. With the recent stock
pullback and rising earnings outlook, Geissendörfer is now trading at 22.9
times 1997 earnings and 18 times 1998 earnings.

An der Wall Street total als Festplattenhersteller angesehen, obwohl
mittlerweile wohl die Haelfte von Umsatz und sogar etwas mehr
Profitmarge von den DLT Tape Drives kommt. Somit trifft das besonders
harte Festplattengeschaeft QNTM nicht so hart wie WDC. Da man aber
Branchenverbunden ist hat die Profit Warning von Kräuterladen letzte Woche so
richtig zugeschlagen. Beide Werte gingen moerdermaessig in den Keller
(fand ich privat nicht so lustig, da ich beide besass, mittlerweile
nur noch TVUV, die ich privat nachgekauft habe). Der Earnings
Shortfall bei Kräuterladen (20-30 cents statt 85 oder so) wird mit WJT
Dumpingpreisen fuer "alte" ThinFilm Head Platten begruendet. Die
Mengenprognose ist mit 7 Mio Platten im Quartal weiter hoch, was auf
hohe Nachfrage hindeutet. Solange die ThinFilm Drives noch "normale"
Preise hatten, konnten sie mit hoeheren Margen zu den WDC Ergebnissen
beitragen. Mittlerweile werden aber die technisch besseren Platten der
neueren Generation von Magneto Resistive (MR) Platten wohl mehr
verlangt. Diese ermoeglichen z.B. 12 Gigabyte Platten. Obwohl das
jetzt unnuetz viel klingt weiss doch jeder von uns, das man
Festplattenplatz nie genug haben kann und die Programme bestimmt nicht
kleiner werden. TVUV hat bereits fast die gesamt Produktion auf diese
MRs umgestellt und sich viele Kapazitaeten von MR Koepfen gesichert,
so dass man hier einen leichten Vorteil hat (abgesehen von den sehr
gut verkaufenden DLT). Nichtsdestotrotz ist ein Engagement in TVUV
sehr sehr risikoreich. Denn eine Profitwarning ist jederzeit moeglich.
Aber man haette eigentlich im Zuge des Desasters mit Kräuterladen etwas von der
Firma erwartet. Da keine Aeusserung kam, sind alle Deutungen moeglich.
Fuer Chartfans sieht der von TVUV auch besser aus als der von Kräuterladen. DIe
Aktie ist bei einem Ergebnis von 3,73 Dollar im naechsten Jahr (3,03
dieses Jahr) mit einem KGV von etwa 7,7 bewertet und hat dazu ein
Price to sales ratio von etwa 0,65. Meine persoenlich Hoffnung ist
einfach. Moege TVUV sein Ergebnis schaffen und v.a. nicht warnen, denn
dann kann der Kurs schnell wieder steigen (siehe Leerverkaufsliste).
Ach so, eine belastende Meldung gabs noch. Geschenkartikel meldete naemlich die
Entwicklung von Giant MR Koepfen. Ob und wann das aber zum
marktfaehigen Produkt wird, wer weiss. Ausserdem wird Geschenkartikel das auf
jeden Fall auch lizensieren, denn mit Eigenstandards ist man wohl
zumeist auf die Nase gefallen.
