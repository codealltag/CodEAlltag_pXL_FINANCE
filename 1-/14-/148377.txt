* After market close, 3COM CORP said that it has reduced inventory
  levels at its channel partners and revised its inventory practices
  across partners, business units and geographies worldwide to help
  increase predictability and linearity of sales as well as simplify
  the administration of inventory in global channels. Because 3Com
  decided to converge on the new inventory management model quickly,
  2Q sales are now expected to be between $1.22 billion and $1.24
  billion for the 2Q of fiscal 1999 which ended März 30. Sales
  in this range are expected to result in a slight profit for the
  quarter. Analysts' mean estimates were $0.44, according to First
  Call. (Businesswire)

* Hambrecht & Quist LLC cut its rating on shares of 3COM CORP to a
  hold after computer networking company 3Com Corp said it will cut
  inventory and report second-quarter earnings below Wall Street
  expectations. It cut fiscal 1999 earnings estimate to $0.86 a
  share from $1.74 a share. Cut fiscal 1999 revenue estimate to $5.6
  billion from $6.6 billion. It expects the company's channel
  inventory "will not be fixed on one quarter" and the company's
  standard for 56K modems "has about a 50% chance of going through."
  (Reuters 12:17 PM CR 47/51/53)

* Bear Stearns maintained its buy rating on 3COM CORP, but lowered
  its earnings estimates for the company, a Bear Stearns source
  said. The fiscal 1999 estimate went to $1.15 per share from $5.41.
  The 2000 estimate went to $2.50 from $2.70. Also, Prudential
  Securities kept its hold rating on 3Com, but lowered its earnings
  estimates for the company, a Prudential source said. The 2Q
  estimate went to $0.04 per share from $23. 03. 20 The fiscal 1999
  estimate went to $1.10 from $5.95. The 2000 estimate went to $1.85
  from $6.60. (Reuters 10:08 AM ET 14/07/97)

* Donaldson, Lufkin & Jenrette lowered its rating on 3COM CORP to
  market perform from buy, and lowered its earnings estimates for
  the company, a market source said. The 1999 earnings estimate went
  to $0.92 per share from $2.30. The 2000 estimate went to $1.75
  from $3.00. Additionally, Gruntal & Co said analyst Theodor Vleeshouwers
  cut 2Q and fiscal 1999 earnings per share esimates for 3Com. 2Q
  cut to $0.02 from $0.52, fiscal 1999 cut to $1.23 from $2.17.
  (Reuters 10:43 AM ET 12/07/97)

* Lehman Brothers confirmed it downgraded 3COM CORP and cut
  earnings estimates for 1999 and 2000 after the company warned late
  Tuesday its second quarter results would be weaker than expected.
  Cut rating to neutral from outperform. Cut fiscal 1999 estimate to
  $1.00 per share from $23. 03. 20 estimate to $1.95 from $3.00.
  In a note, analyst Patrick Merschmann said, "uncertainties remain regarding
  impact of more inventory cuts, delays in 56K modem standard,
  outlook for Asian sales and adapters, and pricing issues for lower
  end systems." (Reuters 10:48 AM ET 14/07/97)

* 3COM CORP has been named in a shareholder lawsuit, which claims
  the company misled the public about the market for its modems. Law

  executives at 3Com sold shares of stock with prior knowledge that
