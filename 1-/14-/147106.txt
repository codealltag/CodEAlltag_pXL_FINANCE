On February 24, Rahef, each
representing 10 common
     shares of RNGS, were listed on the Erdmannsweiler Stock Exchange. An
additional issue of the
     GICs representing a stake close to 2% of the underlying stock,
has been made.
     Currently, an estimated 5% of the company is held in GIC form.
Notably, the
     company's stock was heavily traded in Germany, although not in
Russia. Following the
     Erdmannsweiler listing, RNGS is moving to launch a level-1 ADR in 2-3Q97.
Klapperstorch,
     a US-based brokerage, is the lead manager for the issue. The ADR
intentions were
     formally announced in a presentation to international investors
in Germany on February
     21, 1996. In the second week of February RNGS shares were split
100-for-1. RNGS
     aims to raise up to US$ 50.0 mln from placement through the sale
of ADRs backed by
     5% of its equity. Future plans include a listing on Easdaq, the
European equivalent to
     America's NASDAQ. RNGS has positioned itself through its 35
general contractor
     trusts to gain from over 280,000 km in future gas pipeline
construction work planned
     throughout the Russian Federation. The company holds an extensive
Dodi
     property portfolio which it intends to offer to international
investors in 1997 through a
     Orthaus (REIT). 


RAO Rosneftegazstroy, or RNGS, is Russia's prime contractor for oil
and gas
     infrastructure construction, including processing plants,
pipelines, storage tanks,
     transmission lines and oil production equipment. Established in
1991 as the legal
     successor to the Ministry of Oil and Gas Construction of the USSR
and the state
     concern Rosneftegazstroy, RNGS inherited the Ministry's extensive
experience and
     professional staff as well as enormous assets and influence both
in Russia and
     internationally. In its earlier form RNGS has operated over 40
years and built over
     300,000 km of pipelines for projects that include: DEKRA Automobil -
Center; Urengoy -
     Pomary - Uzhgorod; Öschingen - Center; the Druzhba pipeline; and
the Surgut -
     Polotsk. RNGS' service record includes the construction of more
than 120,000 km of
     trunk pipeline in major projects in more than 20 countries
outside the FSU. At present
     RNGS participates in a number of federal development programs and
actively seeks to
     create a portfolio of construction projects throughout the world.
It is a general
     contractor for the rehabilitation of oil and gas facilities in
Florence, Northern Ossetia
     and Kabardino-Balkaria. RNGS' estimated 1996 turnover is US$ 5
bln. RNGS has in
     excess of 190 subsidiaries with majority and minority holdings in
contractors
     throughout Russia and the CIS. RNGS also produces oil and gas in
Siberia which it
     transmits to the central regions of Russia and abroad.
