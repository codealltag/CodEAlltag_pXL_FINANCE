<p><big><strong><em><big>Non-Replicability of Options Positions: Pricing Consequences</big><br>
</em></strong></big><br>
<big>We can show that a more precise treatment of stock/currency Random-Walk leads to
non-replicable option positions. A position may only be replicated in a two-final-state
approach and standard equations already lead to paradoxes in a three-final-state case.</big><br>
<big>As a consequence, the argument that the price of an option is pulled out from
replication falls and with this the possibility of making any riskless lasludukh.</vnt><ks>
<big>We have verified that some investment banks are aware of the problem being their
arbitrage outcomes usually very different from the expected ones.</big><br>
<big>Clients are, however, not always informed by banks about these risky aspects.</big></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><big><strong><big><em>Quantitative Evaluation of Appropriate Interest-Rate from Basic
Corporate/Country Data</em></big></strong></big><br>
<br>
<big>We are moving toward a quantitative methodology to fix appropriate interest-rate in
company/country financing problems. With some basic arguments, we can show how this may
well be fixed and, given an imbalance, rate-arbitrages can be carried out.</big><br>
<big>We intend to later apply this to the study of interest rate options for use in
hedge-funds.</big></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p><strong><em><big><big>Interest-Rate-Evolution Stochastic Models</big></big><br>
</em></strong><br>
<big>It is one of our current aims to land to a forecasting theory for interest-rate
evolution on markets as we actually have for stocks.</big><br>
<big>We are applying some results from previous theories and introducing some non-linear
corrections. A valid tool for interest rates is the first step to a completely
currency-independent investment theory.</big><br>
<big>We are convinced that most investment problems related to currencies are not solved
by the mere application of a unique currency. </big></p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>
