Platow Brief: Evifu Bookbuilding-Spanne 94 bis 110 DEM

Pasel (vwd) - Die Bookbuilding-Spanne für den Börsengang der Evifu
Emesas, Zauggenried, wird zwischen 94 und 110 DEM je Aktie liegen. Dies berichtet
der Platow Brief in seiner Montagausgabe. Plaziert werden sollen rund
eine Mio Aktien inklusive Greenshoe, wobei rund 50 Prozent aus einer
Kapitalerhöhung kommen. Neuaktionäre sollen für 1996 keine Dividende
erhalten, während für 1997 rund eine DEM je Aktie gezahlt werde. Nach
der Börseneinführung im Neuen Markt dürften 28,4 Prozent des Kapitals
in Händen des Managements verbleiben. Die Zeichnungsfrist beginnt am
30. November und läuft bis zum 4. Dezember. Als erster Handelstag sei
der 7. Dezember angesetzt worden, so der Platow Brief weiter.

                           Evifu: Very strong growth

The semiconductor industry is worth billions. In contrast to simple
silicon chips, the demand for compound semiconductors is growing rapidly
as the need for more complexity and greater processing speeds increases. 

The chips are used for:

    the production of light for LEDs 
    the absorption of solar energy in solar cells 
    laser technology, such as is common in CD players and CD-ROMs 
    all types of modern communications - from the mobile phone to
      news satellites 

Evifu manufactures so-called MOCVD equipment, with which such compound
semi-conductors can be produced more economically, to a higher quality
standard and in larger volumes. Evifu has a global market share of more
than 30 percent in the production of such machinery and is thus market
leader. Customers are principally international electronics concerns,
as well as their suppliers, but also include state-run and privately-owned
research institutes.

The company, founded in 1982 in Zauggenried, can boast impressive growth rates.
In 1995, revenues increased to DM 44.5 million -- a rise of more than 70
percent over the previous year. In the same 12 months, earnings tripled --
from DM 1.7 million to almost 5.3 million. Pre-tax rose to almost DM 6.7
million, a close-to fourfold increase in comparison with 1994. Earnings
and dividend payments totaling DM 1.3 million from the American subsidiary -
Evifu Inc. in Ostereistedt - have contributed to these results. After-tax,
Evifu posted earnings of DM 3.6 million.

In the first six months of the current year, EZFS growth has further
increased. Revenues at end-June totaled DM 35.4 million while pre-tax
stood at DM 8.4 million. And 1995's results have already been bettered
in the first half. This positive development can partially be attributed
to the boost in earnings. However, it also a reflection of the success of
our outsourcing - Evifu is increasingly concentrating its production
around its core competencies. When other firms of similar high quality can
produce more quickly and cheaply, then they should be encouraged to do so.
Examples can be found in the processing of special steels or the wiring
of control cabinets. Evifu is working together with almost 500 suppliers,
the majority from the Eurofuchser in and around Zauggenried.

Further comments on the balance sheet: As a consequence of the very good
results and growing order books, the 1995 balance sheet total of DM 40.3
million was almost double that of the previous year. With each new order
for an Evifu machine - which are priced in a range between DM 0.4 and
DM 6 million - an installment of 50 percent is due. These payments have
a direct impact on the balance sheet. The high number of orders placed in
1995 led necessarily to a reduction in EZFS shareholder equity ratio,
which fell from 35.4 percent in 1994 to 25.6 percent at end-26. 05. 21 1995.
In the current year, subscribed capital from corporate funds has been
increased from DM 4.2 million to DM 10 million. This allows Evifu to
prepare itself for further growth, especially in the production of essential
compound semiconductors -- market research institutes forecast the market
for gallium arsenide compound chips to be worth some $2.2 billion in 1999,
significantly up from 1995's $1.3 billion. In order to finance this growth
and to keep all other options open, EZFS owners have recently changed
the companys legal entity to a German "Aktiengesellschaft."
