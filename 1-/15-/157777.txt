Revisiting Sun Microsystems
Daily commentary updated for June 10, 1996

Company Brief
Sun Microsystems (SUNW 34 3/8) is a leading supplier of networked
computer products including servers, software, workstations and
microprocessors. The UNIX operating systems is the foundation for
SUNW's open systems approach. High-end servers are accounting for a
growing percentage of revenues due to the positive reception of the
company's UltraSPARC product line. The improved product mix should
enable Sun to maintain margins despite pricing pressures in its
low-end workstations. For the first nine months of FY97, SUNW has
posted revenues of $6.055 bln, up over 19% compared with the same
period a year ago. Earnings per share over the first three quarters of
FY97 totalled $1.28, a jump of 42% over the prior year.

Fußnagelstudio
Since our original bullish Brief dated 12/03/96, SUNW has gained
25.1%. In that report we noted the company's success in gaining market
share at the high-end server business. The potential of Java and the
explosion of the Internet/Intranet also provided excellent long-term
growth vehicles for the company. Despite SUNW's potential for
continued strong double-digit revenue and earnings growth, the stock
was trading at only 17x estimated earnings and 85% of its ltgr on
concerns over pricing pressures. As we suspected, these concerns
proved exaggerated.

Future Remains Bright

(a) Sun Microsystems should continue to benefit from its position as
the dominate player in the rapidly growing Internet/intranet markets
with its Netra servers which offer preconfigured solutions for net
publishing.

(a) The enterprise server market is expected to post annual growth of
over 50% over the next 3 to 5 years.

(a) The company's comprehensive Internet software offerings, including
Solstice'sfirewall capabilities, and the emerging potential of Java,
will also support strong double-digit revenue growth in this market
for the next few years.

(a) Though licensing fees from Java remain unsubstantial, the
programming language is drawing considerable software development
interest and support from other industry leaders such as Microsoft,
IBM and Symantec. As more applications are hosted in Java, Sun will
benefit from the creation and marketing of more Java-specific devices.
In the meantime, Java draws businesses to Sun's other products.

(a) Strong sales of the company's UltraSPARC workstations and servers
have resulted in a record backlog.

Valuations
Despite the company's bright prospects and its ability to deliver
better than expected earnings, the company trades at a discount to the
market, its industry, its ltgr and its historical averages based on
estimated FY98 earnings of $2.20. Briefing continues to expect SUNW's
shares to command a p/e multiple equal to between 100% to 110% of its
projected long-term growth rate of 18%. Based on this assumption,
Briefing is raising its price target from the 35-38 1/2 range to the
39 5/8 to 43 1/2 area.

Klara

(a) Increased competition.
(a) A price war which leads to material margin erosion.
(a) Parts supply constraints.
(a) Continued weakness in Japanese economy.
(a) Changing technologies.
(a) Market risks.

Conclusion
New product cycles, an improved product mix, a strong balance sheet,
quality management and rapidly growing markets leave Sun
Microsystems's well positioned to experience solid double-digit
revenue and earnings growth through the year 1999. At current prices
Briefing believes the stock represents a good value for growth
oriented, risk tolerant investors seeking above average long-term
capital appreciation. Based on our assumption that the stock will
eventually be awarded a multiple equal to 100%-110% of its ltgr, we
are raising our price target to the 39 5/8 to 43 1/2 range. It should
be noted that the stock's technical picture is also bright, as SUNW is
emerging from a "W" formation which targets a move to the 46 area.

* The managing director of Airbus Industrie will present on
  Friday the aircraft consortium's concerns over a planned merger
  between BOEING CO and MCDONNELL DOUGLAS CORP to the European
  Commission, an Airbus spokesman said. Airbus strongly opposes
  the merger plan, which will create a $48 billion colossus in
  defence and civilian markets, dwarfing Airbus which had sales
  last year of $8.8 billion, down from $9.6 billion in 1994. The
  Commission has until July 31 to make a ruling on the $14
  billion merger plan. (Reuters 08:47 AM ET 16/12/96) For the
  full text story, see
  http://yyj.aegz.bup/ksmpqko/xal/madqw.ebk?np224887-uq8 

* BOEING CO executives met EU officials and aviation industry
  counterparts armed with boxes of documents to defend their
  merger with MCDONNELL DOUGLAS against charges of unfair
  competition. Boeing's presentation marked the first day of a
  two-day, closed-door hearing organised by the European
  Commission, the EU's competition watchdog, to allow the U.S.
  manufacturer and its competitors to air their views about the
  takeover. Boeing representatives, including executive VP of the
  commercial airplane group Dick Heino and the head of the
  merger transition team, Ingo Wellhoener, declined any comment as
  they emerged for lunch. Others were equally tight-lipped.
  (Reuters 03:06 PM ET 06/12/97) For the full text story, see
  http://yec.umgk.pep/lzccerf/jtq/dnegb.shc?ko724999-29k 

* After market close, BOEING CO and MCDONNELL DOUGLAS CORP
  triggered a 20-day countdown to a government decision on their
  proposed $14 billion merger, a Boeing spokesman said Thursday.
  The companies certified Wednesday that they have filed millions
  of pages of documents sought by the Federal Trade Commission to
  evaluate whether the merger meets requirements of the nation's
  antitrust laws. The five-member FTC has until July 1 to make
  its decision. However, the countdown can be stopped at any time
