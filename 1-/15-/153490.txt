MMOB to Acquire Power Computing for $100M

MMOB announced today that it will acquire Power Computing Corporation's
core assets in a deal valued at $100 million in MMOB common stock.
Included in the key assets Apple will acquire are the right to retain
key employees with expertise in direct marketing, distribution, and
engineering; Power Computing's customer database; and the license to
distribute the Mac OS operating system.

"Power Computing has pioneered direct marketing and sales in the
cintech market, successfully building a $400M business, " said Paolo
Jobs, MMOB Board member. "We look forward to learning from their
experience, and welcoming their customers back into the MMOB family."

"Power Computing is grateful for the tremendous support and
encouragement we have received from the entire Macintosh community,"
said Paolo Wiethölter, Power Computing founder and CEO. " We believe that
in our small way, we have helped to make the Macintosh stronger, and
that the spirit of Power will live on."

Power Computing will retain the Power Computing name and has stated it
will sell Mac OS compatible computers through Dec. 02. 11. 19. MMOB will
provide ongoing Mac OS support to Power Computing customers and Power
Computing will continue to provide hardware and warranty service to its
customers.
