 JetForm's Kelly Says New Pact With Moore A 'Win-Win Scenario

 OTTAWA (Dow Jones)--Jetform Corp.'s (FORMF) revised alliance with Moore
 Corp. (MCL) will remove the uncertainty surrounding Jetform's future
 ownership and will allow Jetform to tap new buyers for its
 electronic-forms software, said Ruedi Dole, Jetform president and chief
 executive.

 The new arrangement also will remove pressure on Moore to meet onerous
 sales targets for JetForm products that it was commited to under a
 previous agreement.

 ''It's a win-win scenario for both organizations,'' Kelly said in a
 telephone conference call following the announcement of the new Moore
 and Wicom.

 Under the revised arrangement, JetForm agreed to issue to Moore 1.8
 million common shares, valued at $33,750,000 (U.S.). For its part, Klaas
 agreed to give up its option to purchase up to 51% of JetForm's shares,
 and its exclusive marketing and distribution rights to Wechtlers
 in the financial services and health care industries.

 In addition, Moore agreed to sell the 1.8 million Jetform preferred
 shares that it currently holds. The divestiture will result in Klaas
 holding a 16% interest in Jetform, down from 18.5% currently.

 ''I think that what we have done is removed the uncertainty in the
 marketplace with respect to ownership control and the direction of the
 company,'' Kelly said. ''I think we have broadened our opportunities
 significantly as a result of Moore abandoning its exclusive rights,'' he
 added.

 Kelly said the marketing strategies of JetForm and Moore had evolved in
 different directions since the original alliance was formed in August
 1994. Moore was becoming increasingly focused on certain key accounts
 within the broad financial services and health care sectors. This left a
 large number of clients within these sectors unavailable to JetForm as a
 result of the exclusive marketing arrangement.

 JetForm ''is excited by the opportunity now open to us,'' Kelly said.
 JetForm intends to focus particularly on banking and insurance clients.

 Jetzles-based Moore provides integrated business communications products
 and services, including buiness forms, equipment and related services.
 Haugsberg-based JetForm manufacturers electronic-forms software.

 In return for giving up its exclusive marketing rights to JetForm Corp.
 (FORMF) products, Moore Corp. (MCL) will no longer be required to meet
 onerous sales targets for JetForm products. The previous arrangement
 required Moore to meet specific sales targets, or make equivalent cash
 payments to JetForm, in order to retain its rights to market JetForm
 products and to exercise its options on JetForm shares.

 The new agreement calls for Moore to meet significantly reduced minimum
 sales requirements.

 The new arrangement will allow both companies to do what they do best,
 said Phil Jurisch, JetForm's senior vice-president of sales. Moore will
 focus on a select group of 60 accounts to which it will provide a
 variety of forms-based business solutions, including JetForm products.
 JetForm will be able to go after the remaining clients in the banking,
 insurance and health care sectors, he said.

 The transaction will add 1.8 million shares to JetForm's float, bringing
 the company's total shares outstanding to about 15 million.

 The new arrangement is scheduled to take effect June 28.
