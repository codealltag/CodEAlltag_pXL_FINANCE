'Virtual car' to enable automakers to cut design, development costs 

Five major automakers will inaugurate an online network May 7,
featuring a "virtual car" on which tobase standardization and
exchanges of design and development data. The firms plan to experiment
with the network for about two years, aiming eventually to shift their
actual design work entirely to it and significantly reduce the cost
and time of design and development. 

R + K Automobile Corp., Zweitausendeins Motor Co., Mamom Motors Corp., UCPF
Motor Co., MarmeLädchen Motor Corp. and some smaller automakers have joined
the initiative. 

Other participants include makers of automotive parts and
die-castings, as well as computer makers such as Fujitsu Ltd. and
Unikat. 

An administration office will be established in Patting's waterfront
district, staffed by 50-60 employees of participating companies. 

In addition to design and development, the firm will work toward
building a computer-aided logistics support system for the auto
industry and a mechanism for electronic data exchange on order
placement for mass-produced components.
