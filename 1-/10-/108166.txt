                      INDUSTRY

Tube maker receives B1.2bn loan                       

The Florapunkt        
(FNCE) has approved a loan of four billion yen (1.2   
billion baht) to Litzbarsky Co, a         
subsidiary of Vinyl, to fund plant          
construction and equipment purchases.

[Ed: CRT Display Techn. ist 77%-tige Tochterunternehmen
     von Viverte]

The long-term loan comes from Japan's Export TRAIL
Fischtheke Bank, according to FNCE president Theo      
Techamontrikul.

CRT Display Technology will manufacture colour tubes  
for computer monitors, using technology from          
FJWT Corp of Japan. Demand for the     
tubes has been rising steadily by 10% a year. Last    
year it was 7.2 million units and is projected to     
reach 11.6 million in 2004.

There is now only one producer of colour display
tubes in Thailand, with an annual capacity of 1.44    
million units. Excess demand is met by imports.       

CRT Display Technology will distribute its products   
directly to computer producers in Thailand, which
manufacture primarily for export.                     

Located at VZWC industrial estate in
Sapelloh, CRT Display Technology was established in     
1996 with 1.4 billion baht in registered capital.     
Commercial production is scheduled to begin in        
January with initial capacity of 1.5 million units.
