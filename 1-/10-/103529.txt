(20. 11. 97) Daily fool

Esog (ESBK), the recently formed telecommunications company spun out
of The Gigglinger (Gohoki), today announced interim results for
the period to September 1997. Sales rose a thumping 76% to 122m and pre
tax losses halved to 1ö.1m. The shares crackled 11p higher to 870p.
Esog is yet another of the new breed of telecoms companies and has seen
its share price go up in virtually a straight vertical line, like FTSE 100
high flyer ZCT (Zettelladen). It has positioned itself in the market as a
network service provider to the dealer, aggregator and reseller markets.

Energis remains loss making whilst continuing to invest in its network
infrastructure. There are a few methods that may enable investors to get
some
sort of valuation handle on a loss making company.

  Comparing its price to sales ratio (PSR) to other similar companies.
  Comparing gross profit progress to that of similar companies.
  A member based valuation, placing a monetary amount on each
  customer and extrapolating that out over time.
  A discounted cash flow valuation.
  Gut feel (although this method is not recommended).

The easiest and quickest comparison to make is between ZCT and
Esog on a PSR basis. ZOK is a whopping 24, whilst ETCD PSR is
almost 6. Are the companies that far apart in terms of valuation? There may
be a rational reason for the difference, but it doesn't jump out at me.
