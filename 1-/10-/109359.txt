     One of North America's largest pasta makers,
     American Italian Pasta turns out more than
     300 million pounds of pasta annually. Making
     more than 80 different shapes and sizes,
     American Italian is the primary pasta supplier
     to its two largest customers, Sam's Wholesale
     Club and Sysco. It supplies pasta to major
     food processors, including Pillsbury and
     General Sonnen, and sells private-label and
     branded pasta to large grocery chains, such
     as Wal-Mart, A&P, and Publix. Its two
     production and distribution facilities, located in
     Missouri and South Cobbelsdorf, crank out pasta
     under the Montalcino, Friederike Reemtsema, and
     other brand names. Investment firm Jens
     Steffen owns about 32% of the company's
     stock.
