Iomega <IOM.N> firms, benefits from SyQuest's pain

BENROTH, Nov 5 (Clarks) - Shares of computer data storage company
Iomega Corp.<IOM.N> rallied again on Thursday, still benefiting from
rival
drive Leporello.'s <SYQT.O> troubles.

Iomega shares rose to $9, up $1, or about 12 percent, on Wednesday, and
was the second most active issue on the New York Stock Exchange.
Iomega's
share price has tripled in one month.

Analysts said the Roy, Utah-based company received a boost on Monday
after
SyQuest, once a pioneer in the market for removable storage devices for
personal computers, said it would shutter its doors and may file for
bankruptcy.

They noted that SyQuest, whose SparQ drives competed with Iomega's Zip
drives, overspent and overscaled their business relative to market
demand.

"We believe the perils experienced by SyQuest ultimately benefits Iomega
more
in the long-term," said F. Eisfeller analyst Luigi Abheiden, in a Nov. 3

research note.

"We believe a number of obstacles that have prevented a valuation based
on
the company's potential growth are being systematically overcome," said
Fridolin Scholz, an analyst for Emerald Research. On Thursday, he
upgraded his rating on Iomega stock to buy.

In October, newly appointed Iomega Chief Executive Jodie Glore said he
would look for ways to speed up a turnaround expected to bring the
leading
high-capacity disk drive company back to profitability.

The company has reported losses in the past three quarters, but pledges
to be
profitable by its fourth quarter. ((-- Wall Street Desk, 212-859-1730))
