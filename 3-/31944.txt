Title: 
Modeling Complex Dynamic Real-Life Systems:
Example-based Forecast of Market Trends for DAX Options 
ABSTRACT: 
As an example of modeling complex dynamic real-life systems, like
those represented by the widespread "marketplace pattern", dynamic
market trends (DMT) for DAX options are forecasted. 
While option prices often are said to be "random" and most existing
option pricing models do not show significant predictive capabilities,
analysis of option price time series, according to nonlinear dynamic
system theory, gives ground to suppose a considerable amount of
determinism in option markets, if understood as auto-adaptive
non-stationary dynamic systems, exposed to several "external
disturbances", e.g. from economy, politics, etc.
