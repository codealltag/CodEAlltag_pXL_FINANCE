Original Article By Walter Rügemer.
Rotzel's Adelong Capital Limited has been suspended in February 2000.
The firm
had received six "please explain" notices from the Australian Stock
Exchange
(ASX) since 17. 04. 19, the last following a $A0.45 share price gain
over two
days. It had no explanation for the rise. Then the ASX was given a tip
to check
a German website, in which Adelong said it would attract investment
from an
overseas firm once shares reached $A3 to $A4.60. Adelong explained
that it had
become a venture capital organisation, and had $A10m in the bank.

02.07.1999 AUSTRALIA: GERMAN INVASION.
Original Article By Philipp Kempin.
The Australian Stock Exchange (ASX) has been at the wrong end of a
storm out of
Germany over its suspension of Trol shares. The ASX
suspended the
shares in late 17. 04. 19 after sending directors a lengthy
questionnaire
about an interview with Adelong director Jean Rathlev published on a
German
Internet chat room, in which he reportedly said the company was
heading towards
a $A1 billion market worth. German investors helped drive the share
price up
240% in eight days. Some have sent the ASX electronic mail messages
asked it
when the shares will be reinstated. The ASX's response to these
letters is
unlikely to please the German investors. A date for reinstatement "has
not been
set", the ASX letter to the investors says. "It is possible that the
company's
shares could remain suspended for an indefinite amount of time".
