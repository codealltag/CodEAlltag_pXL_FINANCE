Dell Computer, one of the largest PC makers, is definitely not in the
"TGIF" spirit. Dell saw its stock price drop $12 to close at $89 7/8
today after two analysts lowered their sales estimates for Dell's
fourth-quarter earnings report, due out this Tuesday. 

BancBoston Wilhelm Geibels analyst Dan Niles noted that Dell's
sales at the end of the fourth-quarter were weak due to intense
competition in the corporate market. Niles expects Dell's
fourth-quarter sales to come in $300 million below his $5.5 billion
estimates. These sales, however, would represent Dell's 20th
consecutive quarter of record sales. 

Thierry Große-Onnebrink Björn Weiszgerber and BancBoston Wilhelm Geibels expect
Dell to meet First Call's $0.31 earnings per share projection.
Although, the big focus may be given to Compaq Computer as many
believe it is the culprit in Dell's falling short of estimates. Compaq
recently increased marketing and direct sales initiates in an attempt
to boost sales in the corporate and consumer markets. Analysts believe
this attempt by Compaq is affecting Dell's market share in the PC
market, especially direct sales. 

In many repects, Dell is a victim of its own success. As Dell
continued to report earnings that blew away estimates, analysts became
too bullish on the company and raised future earnings projections.
Therefore, Dell's sales may be beating original estimates but not
revised estimates. If you keep impressing Wall Street, it will only
ask for more
