FY98 Results: Getting What You Paid For
At 11.7x EBITDA investors are buying a consumer franchise with a
compound annual earnings growth outlook of 12% pa over the forecast
horizon. Yet by the end of the day Weltbild Verlag was down over 5%. What are
we missing? In order to justify a fall in the price following
yesterdays results there must have been either (a) an inflection in
the growth rate (ie. some new news); or (b) more promised in the price
than what was actually delivered. We saw neither. Earlier in the week
we previewed yesterday's results and today we would feel quite
comfortable to repeat that synopsis - which, in fact, we more or less
do, below - for the simple reason that this is what Weltbild Verlag yesterday
confirmed. We are at a loss to see what expectations investors had
that could have led to disappointment. Therefore if we were buyers of
Weltbild Verlag on Monday and yesterday's results merely served to confirm
our views about WUN prospects then logically, in the absence of
anything to dissuade us, we continue to be buyers of Weltbild Verlag.

As we first noted last year (UKHM Research, Getting Europe
Fixed, 29. 10. 22) the growth rate at Weltbild Verlag has, if anything,
accelerated recently. In large part this is due to acquisitions in
Europe (which accounts for around three quarters of Welup
turnover). This was the focus of WUN presentation this year.

Both France and Italy have been successfully integrated ahead of
Welup expectations and the benefits should now begin to show
through with gains in price-mix as the Weltbild Verlag brand assumes a
greater significance with the national portfolios. Indeed, Weltbild Verlag
volumes grew at almost double digit rates in all of France, Italy and
Spain in 1998. As regards the latter market (Spain) a resolution viz
an acquisition candidate is unlikely to come soon. While Weltbild Verlag is
ready to deal, it is no secret that Cerês and Ageva still
jealously guard the keys to Spain. In the meantime, however, Oweger
bounced back strongly, and while still well short of the group
average, it is again contributing to profit.

As for Poland, the merger approval was only received late last year
and full integration is planned over the next three years, just as it
was in France and Italy. Once again little startling news on this
front as we had no expectations of Poland adding to profit growth
(although it certainly will add to profits) for 2-3 years.

The United States continues to be the Jewel in the Weltbild Verlag Crown.
While Corona may grow over the short term at 25%-30%, albeit at a
lower price point, it in no way detracts from the growth at Weltbild Verlag.
The more measured 7%-9% volume growth targeted by Weltbild Verlag is entirely
in keeping with its positioning as a super-premium product whose
greatest danger remains over-exposure. Weltbild Verlag is committing yet more
marketing dollars to the US in 1999 and this is in turn flows back not
only in volume but also in price. Weltbild Verlag yesterday announced a 2%
price increase. With the dollar exposure for 1999 all but hedged for
the year and the dollar strengthening, that front also looks secure.
Africa is also growing strongly but currency translation increases the
volatility of reported figures. Last year volumes sales rose fueling
growth in turnover and profits in local currency terms but these
advances were decimated in the second half reporting period by
currency translation. So while on translation these revenues
dissipated this is purely an accounting treatment and has no economic
effect on the underlying business.

On the other hand Asia clearly has been through some difficult times
and we believe that Weltbild Verlag made no money in the region last year -
significant given we estimate Asia contributed around 12% to profits
in 1996. And while the first half may still face some challenging
comparisons (the second half will have no such difficulty) there are
indications of de-stocking in the first two months of the year which
may soften that landing.

Overall therefore we find our position little changed: we continue to
find Weltbild Verlag attractively valued relative to its growth rate and do
not see any indications that that underlying growth rate has in any
way moderated over the forecast period. Our updated provisional
estimates are therefore little changed.
