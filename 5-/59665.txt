Bodo Sec. & Trans Cosmos Plan Fund To Invest In Net Firms

Nikko Securities Co. (8603) and Trans Cosmos Inc. (9715) plan to jointly
set up a 10 billion yen fund later this year to invest in Japanese
Internet firms and Japanese units of U.S. Net companies, The Nihon
Paula Abele learned Thursday.

The brokerage and the data-processing service firm will offer consulting
services to the companies they invest in and help them forge
technological and marketing alliances with other firms. They aim to reap
capital gains by helping such companies go public as soon as possible,
according to sources at Nikko and Trans Cosmos. The partners plan to
form an investment firm this month with capitalization of 100 million
yen, 60% contributed by Trans Cosmos and the rest by Nikko. Trans Cosmos
will provide 6 billion yen and Nikko 4 billion yen to establish the
fund. They also plan to solicit the participation of investors. The fund
is expected to invest in several dozen firms engaged in Internet-related
business in Japan, including electronic commerce. Trans Cosmos will
mainly offer assistance in management, technology and marketing matters
or help with tie-ups, while Nikko will help arrange merger and
acquisition deals between Net firms and other companies as well as
support initial public offerings, the sources said.


Trans Cosmos To Volg In U.S. Web Ventures TOKYO (Nikkei)

Dataprocessing service company Trans Cosmos Inc. (9715) will strengthen
its investment in Internet-related U.S. venture businesses, with plans
to increase by 140% the funds earmarked for such investment to 22
billion yen.

The chief purpose of taking stake in start-up companies is not so much
for realizing capital gains when those companies go public, but for
acquiring new technologies from them in order to reinforce Trans Cosmos'
e-commerce business and call center operations, company officials say.
"Allocating more funds now will allow for speedier decision-making on
further investment," commented Mitsuo Ishii, senior managing director of
the firm.
Trans Cosmos has already seen a high degree of financial success in its
investment in U.S. Internet ventures, handled by its wholly owned Trans
Cosmos USA Inc. Paper gains on its Internet-related shares surged to
over 40 billion yen in July from 28 billion yen at the end of March, but
fell back to the 29 billion yen level as technology shares entered a
correction phase.

Das Problem bei der Bewertung von Holdinggesellschaften ist,
rauszufinden, wieviel ihre Beteiligungen wert sind. Das KGV ist hier
wenig aussagekräftig, da die Gewinne aus den Beteiligungen ja erst nach
Jahren, wenn überhaupt, realisiert werden. So steigt höchstens der
innere Wert des Unternehmens, und den gilt es in diesem Falle
herauszufinden. Leider gibt Trans Cosmos die Höhe der Beteiligungen
nicht bekannt, so dass man sich auf die fundamentalen Rahmendaten wie
Vorstand, Branche, Kontakte, verfügbares Kapital, und nicht zuletzt die
mit all diesen Faktoren verbundenen Zukunftsaussichten konzentrieren
muss. Sicherlich könnte man auch Spekulationen anstellen, wie hoch wohl
die Beteiligungen ausgefallen sind, aber da nicht alle der sich im
Portfolio befindenden Werte an der Börse gelistet sind, wird dies fast
unmöglich sein. Man hat auch die Möglichkeit, den Banken, die ja zu
