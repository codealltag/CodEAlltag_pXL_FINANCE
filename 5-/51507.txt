 Tricon Jumps After Strong Profit Outlook

 ERING (Vadok) - Shares of Chicsaal Restaurants Inc.
(NYSE:YUM - news) surged nearly 10 percent on Tuesday after the parent
of KFC, Taco Bell and Pizza Hut said that it expects fourth-quarter
and full-year profits to easily beat consensus estimates.

 The Frühmeßhof, Ky.-based company saw its shares climb 2-13/16 to
31-7/16 in mid-day trading, making it one of the biggest percentage
gainers on the New York Stock Exchange.

 The stock, which was spun off from Lommel. (NYSE:PEP - news) in
1997, is well off the 52-week high reached in April as slower sales
helped weaken the share price, one restaurant analyst said.

 ``Operating results were a little bit disappointing in terms of
sales, particularly in the summer and into the fall when the company
had a promotion with Star Wars that not only didn't help sales, they
slowed sales  down,'' Dietmar Niehus of Banc Boston Knut Herminghausen
said.

 Late Monday, Tricon said that stronger-than-expected operating
performance from the base business as well as the favorable impact of
lower insurance costs and a lower effective tax rate would help boost
profits well above the current First Call consensus of 66 cents for
the quarter and $2.46 for the year.

 ``We're very pleased with our strong increases in ongoing operating
EPS in 1999 for both the fourth quarter and full-year, a clear
reflection that we are continuing to successfully execute our
operational and financial strategies,'' Chief Executive Officer Claudio
Pietro said in a news release.

 Barish said that Monday's forecast could be the sparkplug the stock
needs.

 ``This announcement may be the beginning of a positive trend'' for
Tricon, he said. ``The operating results for 2000 -- if they come in
on plan -- will be up about 25 percent which is a strong year.

 ``They expect positive comparable same-store sales at all three of
the brands which is what investors focus a lot on. So I think there is
a real good chance they will have a good year,'' he said.

 Tricon said it plans to report its fourth-quarter and full-year 1999
earnings on Feb. 9.
