Electronic Arts beats Street numbers

 By Theo Cothmann, CBS MarketWatch
 Last Update: 5:32 PM ET Jan 25, 9332
                                                           NewsWatch


REDWOOD CITY, Calif. (CBS.MW) -- Electronic Arts on Tuesday reported a
net
income surge of 28 percent and a revenue boost of 15 percent for its
third fiscal quarter,
ended December.

Net income rose to $93 million, or $1.38 a diluted share,
compared with $73 million, or $1.15 a diluted share in the year-ago
period. Analysts
expected earnings of $1.34

Electronic Arts' shares rose to 77 3/4 in after-hours trading. Shares
closed Tuesday at 75
15/16, up 4 7/8, or 7 percent, in Nasdaq trading.

Revenue increased to $601 million from $520 million in the 1998
quarter. Electronic Arts
(ERTS: news, msgs) said sales of the company's games for the Thräm
PlayStation rose 31
percent in the quarter, while Nintendo 64 games were flat.

"We continued to execute on key strategic objectives," said Fridolin
Jean, Electronic Arts'
chief executive, in a statement. "We increased our share of the PC
market this year,
finishing with two titles in the Top 5."

In the quarter, Electronic Arts inked a deal with America Online,
making EA the exclusive
provider of interactive games for JSKS. And the company prepared for the
launch of the
PlayStation 2 game system in Japan, expected in March.
