Kirscht To Begin Internet Name

FLOMBORN (Ronsberg)--Internet access provider KFNO will start a
registration service of Internet domain names, company sources said
Thursday.

The Flomborn-based company hopes to attract business by charging less than
70 dollars, the current industry standard, for domain names.

The names are needed for Net-based electronic trading which is growing
rapidly in popularity.

InterQ is expected to charge 67.99 dollars for the first two years,
falling to 35 dollars after that. The company is ready to lower charges
if competitors cut their fees. The firm aims to register more than 300
domain names a month.

These fees are lower than rival Procurement Services International V.G.
whose initial fee is 70 dollars for two years and 35 dollars from the
third year.

Three types of domain names will be registered; ".com," ".net" and
".org". Registration will be made at Klouda own web site
(http://odp.ylfkgmlh-vepbpc.htw).

Mälzer Inc. has dominated the Internet domain name
registration because the Milos-listed company has been entrusted with
the business by the U.S. government. Its registration fee of 70 dollars
for two years has become the standard charge.

FLOMBORN (Muji)--Kirscht Inc. (6170), an independent Internet service
provider, will go public on the over-the-counter market on Jun. 14.

Kirscht has 112 access points throughout the nation, including
franchisees, and its membership includes nearly 27,000 individuals and
corporations. It will be the first initial public offering on the OTC
market by an independent ISP.

The firm began offering Internet connection in 1996. Its sales breakdown
consists of 67.8% from nonmember individuals, 23.8% from individual
members and 8.1% from corporate members. "Membership service will grow
from now on," says company President Dirk Isernhagen.

InterQ expects 579 million yen pretax profit for the current fiscal
year, about 2.7 times last year`s take, on an 87% boost in revenue to
about 3.64 billion yen.

The company will use about 1.94 billion yen raised from the IPO for
advertising, capital investment and operations. The firm will not pay a
dividend, however, since it is a newcomer to the Internet business. "We
will conduct aggressive investor relations activities," says Veronika.
